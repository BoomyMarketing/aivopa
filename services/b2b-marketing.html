B2B Services

B2B Marketing Channels We Run

B2B marketing requires a different channel mix than B2C. Your buyers are researching on Google, scrolling LinkedIn, attending virtual events, and reading industry publications — not impulse-buying on TikTok. We deploy paid media where B2B buyers actually are, with messaging designed for business case justification, not emotional response.

💼

LinkedIn Advertising

LinkedIn is the only platform where you can target by company size, job title, seniority level, industry, and specific company name simultaneously. We run Sponsored Content (thought leadership and direct-response feed ads), Lead Gen Forms (native forms that convert at 3x website landing pages because they pre-fill LinkedIn profile data), InMail campaigns for outbound-style warm intro messages, and retargeting of LinkedIn company page visitors and event attendees. ABM-targeted LinkedIn campaigns reaching specific companies' decision-makers are the highest-ROI B2B channel for deals over $25K ACV.

Best for: SaaS, professional services, enterprise, B2B lead gen
🔍

B2B Paid Search

Google and Microsoft Ads capture buyers actively searching for your solution category — the highest-intent traffic in B2B. We target three query types: category keywords (buyers defining the solution type — 'project management software'), competitor keywords (buyers evaluating alternatives — 'Salesforce alternative'), and integration/trigger keywords (buyers in specific technical contexts — 'Hubspot CRM integration'). B2B search requires dedicated landing pages by keyword cluster, ad copy that addresses ICP pain points, and lead qualification questions in the conversion flow — not generic contact forms.

Best for: High-intent buyers, competitive displacement, solution-aware searches
🎯

Account-Based Marketing (ABM)

ABM focuses your marketing budget on a defined list of high-value target accounts — coordinating paid media, content, and sales outreach specifically to decision-makers at companies in your ICP. We build ABM programs using LinkedIn company targeting (reaching all relevant job titles at specific companies), programmatic IP targeting (serving display ads to visitors from target company IP ranges), and intent data platforms (Bombora, G2, TechTarget) to identify accounts showing research behavior for your category. ABM works best for deals over $50K ACV where the CAC of account-level marketing is justified by deal economics.

Best for: Enterprise deals, strategic accounts, long sales cycles
🔄

B2B Retargeting

B2B retargeting keeps your brand visible during the extended consideration window — typical B2B buyers evaluate solutions for 3–6 months before purchasing. We retarget website visitors with LinkedIn matched audiences (serving ads to people who visited your site when they're back on LinkedIn), Google Display remarketing (staying present across the web as they continue researching), and programmatic display retargeting (reaching them on industry publications and professional content sites). B2B retargeting creative focuses on proof — case studies, ROI statistics, customer logos — not promotional offers.

Best for: Long sales cycles, multi-stakeholder buying committees
📄

Content Syndication & Intent Data

Content syndication distributes your gated content (whitepapers, research reports, buyer guides) through third-party publisher networks (TechTarget, IDG, Foundry, NetLine) where B2B buyers actively research their category. Leads are generated when buyers download your content on these platforms — with verified business email and job title. We combine content syndication with intent data from Bombora to target accounts showing research behavior for your category keywords — timing outreach when buyer intent signals are highest and purchase likelihood is greatest.

Best for: MQL generation, early-funnel nurture, category education

Our Process

B2B Demand Generation Methodology

B2B marketing failure usually traces back to one of three problems: wrong ICP targeting, wrong message for the funnel stage, or broken attribution that hides what's actually working. Our process starts by fixing all three before spending a dollar on paid media.

01

ICP Definition & Account Segmentation

We start with your ideal customer profile — the specific company attributes (industry, company size, technology stack, revenue, growth stage), buyer personas (job titles, responsibilities, KPIs, pain points), and deal characteristics (typical ACV, sales cycle length, decision-making process) that define your best customers. ICP clarity is the foundation of everything — wrong ICP means you're generating leads that will never become revenue, regardless of how efficiently you generate them. We map your ICP to platform targeting capabilities: which LinkedIn company sizes and job titles, which Google keyword clusters, which intent data signals, and which content topics align with each ICP segment.

02

Funnel Stage Content Mapping

B2B buyers need different content at different stages of their decision journey. Problem-aware (top of funnel): educational content establishing the cost of the problem you solve — industry reports, benchmark data, thought leadership. Solution-aware (middle of funnel): content positioning your solution category as the answer — comparison guides, methodology explainers, product demos. Vendor-aware (bottom of funnel): content justifying your specific product over alternatives — case studies, ROI calculators, customer proof, competitive battle cards. We map your existing content to these stages, identify gaps, and develop a content brief for each gap before launching paid media — because the right content for the right funnel stage is what converts click into SQL.

03

Channel Architecture & Budget Allocation

We design your B2B channel mix based on deal size, target audience, and budget: high-intent paid search for buyers actively researching (Google Ads, Microsoft Ads), LinkedIn for reaching buyers not yet searching but matching your ICP, retargeting to keep engaged visitors in consideration, and ABM display for strategic account coverage. Budget allocation follows a funnel logic: more budget to bottom-funnel (highest intent, closest to purchase), moderate budget to mid-funnel (consideration stage, retargeting), and awareness-stage LinkedIn only when bottom-funnel channels are saturated. We don't run awareness-stage brand campaigns before demand capture is fully funded.

04

Landing Page & Conversion Architecture

B2B landing pages are where most campaigns fail — sending high-intent traffic to generic homepages, using vague CTAs ('Contact Us' instead of 'See Pricing'), or requiring too much friction in the conversion form (12-field forms vs. 3-field LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms). We audit and optimize your entire conversion path: ad-to-landing-page message match, headline specificity (does it address the exact pain point of the ad's audience?), social proof positioning (customer logos, case study stats, review ratings), form field optimization (fewer fields = more completions, qualification happens in the sales call), and follow-up speed (B2B leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to enter the sales cycle).

05

Lead Scoring & Sales Handoff

Not all leads are equal — a VP of Engineering at a 500-person SaaS company downloading your whitepaper is a very different lead than a freelancer filling out a contact form. We configure lead scoring in your CRM based on: demographic fit (company size, job title match to ICP), behavioral signals (pages visited, content downloaded, email engagement), and channel source (intent-signal leads from G2 or Bombora score higher than generic LinkedIn Sponsored Content leads). Leads above the SQL threshold route automatically to sales with context: which company, which pages they visited, which content they downloaded, and which campaign generated the lead — so sales has context for the first call.

06

Pipeline Attribution & Monthly Reporting

Monthly B2B marketing reports cover the metrics that connect marketing spend to business outcomes: cost-per-MQL by channel (volume and quality), MQL-to-SQL conversion rate by channel (which channels generate leads that sales actually accepts), cost-per-SQL by channel (your real acquisition cost for qualified pipeline), pipeline generated (total contract value of SQLs by marketing source), and closed-won revenue attributed to marketing. We present a full funnel view — not just ad platform metrics — because B2B marketing ROI lives in the revenue your campaigns generate, not the impressions they buy.

FAQ

B2B Marketing Questions Answered

What is the difference between B2B and B2C marketing?
B2B marketing targets businesses as buyers — longer sales cycles (30–180+ days), multiple decision-makers (avg. 6.8 stakeholders), higher deal values, and purchase decisions driven by ROI and business case rather than emotion. B2B channels emphasize professional networks (LinkedIn), intent-based search, and direct outreach, with content that builds credibility over time (case studies, whitepapers, demos) rather than optimizing for impulse purchase. Attribution is more complex — a B2B sale may involve 15+ touchpoints over 6 months before a contract is signed.
What B2B marketing channels drive the best pipeline?
Channel effectiveness varies by deal size. For SMB SaaS (ACV under $15K): Google Ads on commercial-intent queries, LinkedIn Ads for brand building, and retargeting. For mid-market ($15K–100K ACV): LinkedIn ABM targeting specific company sizes and job titles, content syndication on intent platforms (G2, TechTarget), and Google Ads for branded and competitor terms. For enterprise ($100K+ ACV): LinkedIn ABM coordinated with SDR outreach, executive-targeted display retargeting, and thought leadership amplification to buying committee members.
What is account-based marketing (ABM) and when should I use it?
ABM treats individual high-value companies as markets of one — coordinating personalized marketing and sales outreach to the buying committee at target accounts. ABM is right when: deal size exceeds $50K ACV, you have a defined ICP with a manageable list of target accounts (100–500), and your sales team is aligned to work named accounts alongside marketing. ABM requires sales-marketing alignment — marketing generates account-level awareness and engagement signals, sales converts them.
How do you measure B2B marketing ROI?
B2B marketing ROI requires measuring pipeline and closed revenue contribution — not just leads. Metrics that matter: MQLs, SQLs, SQOs (sales-qualified opportunities), pipeline generated (contract value), pipeline won (closed revenue from marketing-sourced pipeline), and cost-per-SQL. We set up CRM attribution tracking (HubSpot, Salesforce) to track marketing touchpoints through the entire sales cycle — so you see which campaigns generate SQLs that close, not just which campaigns generate the most leads.
What budget do I need for B2B paid media?
Minimum viable B2B paid media: LinkedIn Ads ($5,000+/mo for meaningful professional audience reach), Google Ads for B2B keywords ($3,000+/mo — B2B keywords cost $10–50+ CPC), programmatic ABM display ($2,000+/mo for account-level targeting). For a comprehensive demand gen program across LinkedIn, Google, and retargeting, budget $10,000–25,000/mo in ad spend. Below $5,000/mo total, B2B paid media rarely generates sufficient pipeline to justify the investment over organic and outbound alternatives.
