Formats

Programmatic Channels We Manage

Programmatic is not one channel — it's a buying mechanism that spans display, video, audio, CTV, and out-of-home. Each format reaches your audience in a different context, at a different cost, and with different creative requirements. We select and blend formats based on your funnel stage and conversion goal.

📺

Connected TV (CTV)

Non-skippable video ads on streaming platforms — Roku, Fire TV, Hulu, Peacock, Paramount+, and Samsung TV Plus. Reaches cord-cutters (68% of US households) with TV-quality creative and digital-precision targeting. No minimum GRP commitments, no upfront negotiations — pure programmatic with full attribution.

Best for: DTC brands, high-AOV products, local market targeting
🖥️

Display Programmatic

Banner, rich media, and native ads across 2M+ premium publishers via Google DV360, The Trade Desk, or Basis. We use first-party data onboarding, contextual targeting, and lookalike modeling to reach your exact audience wherever they browse — not just on Google and Meta properties.

Best for: Retargeting, prospecting, brand reinforcement at scale
🎬

Online Video (OLV)

Pre-roll, mid-roll, and outstream video ads across premium video content outside YouTube — news sites, sports properties, lifestyle publishers. We access private marketplace deals with top-tier publishers for brand-safe, high-viewability video inventory at CPMs unavailable through open auction.

Best for: Brand awareness, product launches, high-consideration buyers
🎧

Digital Audio

Programmatic audio ads on Spotify, Pandora, iHeartRadio, and podcast networks. Reaches audiences in screen-free moments — commuting, exercising, cooking — when other digital channels can't compete. Audio ads have 24% higher brand recall than display and strong completion rates due to format non-skippability.

Best for: Mass-market brands, local targeting, high-frequency messaging
🏙️

Digital OOH (DOOH)

Programmatic buys on digital billboards, transit screens, retail displays, and venue screens. Target specific locations, dayparts, and weather conditions — serve different creative to NYC commuters at 8am vs. LA shoppers at 2pm. Full attribution via mobile device ID matching to website visits.

Best for: Local market campaigns, event targeting, retail foot traffic
📰

Native Advertising

In-feed native units that match the look and feel of publisher content — sponsored article links, recommendation widgets, and in-feed social units. Native ads generate 53% more views than banner ads and 18% higher purchase intent. We manage native programmatically via Taboola, Outbrain, and DSP native inventory.

Best for: Content promotion, mid-funnel nurture, high-consideration products

Our Methodology

Performance-First Programmatic Strategy

Most programmatic agencies optimize for impressions, viewability, and brand lift metrics. We optimize for revenue. Every programmatic campaign we manage has hard ROAS or cost-per-acquisition targets — and the attribution infrastructure to prove whether we're hitting them.

01

Audience Architecture & Data Strategy

We build your programmatic audience stack in three layers: first-party data (email lists, CRM segments, website visitors onboarded via CDPs or identity resolution), second-party data (partner data from inventory-specific sources like CTV viewership), and third-party data (in-market segments, interest-based, demographic). We map each audience to a funnel stage, a format, and a bid price — so every impression serves a purpose in the purchase journey.

02

DSP Selection & Inventory Strategy

We work across Google DV360, The Trade Desk, and Basis DSP — selecting the platform based on your campaign objectives and inventory access needs. CTV inventory often requires The Trade Desk for premium deal access. Google Display works through DV360 for unified measurement. We negotiate private marketplace (PMP) deals with premium publishers for guaranteed brand-safe inventory at better CPMs than open auction.

03

Attribution & Measurement Setup

Programmatic attribution is complex — view-through conversions, cross-device paths, and long consideration windows require a thoughtful measurement framework. We configure view-through windows appropriate to your purchase cycle (1-day for impulse, 7-day for considered purchases, 30-day for high-ticket B2B), cross-device attribution via identity resolution, and incrementality testing to isolate programmatic's true conversion lift from organic baseline.

04

Creative Specifications & Brand Safety

Each programmatic format has strict creative specifications — HTML5 display banners (300x250, 728x90, 160x600, 320x50), 15/30-second CTV videos (MP4, MOV), audio 30-second spots (MP3/WAV), and OLV 6/15/30-second videos. We brief and QA creative against specs before trafficking, and configure brand safety controls: content category exclusions, domain blocklists, and viewability thresholds (min. 60% in-view for display, 100% completion for CTV).

05

Campaign Optimization & Budget Allocation

Weekly pacing reviews ensure budget is deploying efficiently without over- or under-delivery. We optimize audience segments by CPM and conversion rate, shift budgets toward high-performing inventory packages, and refresh creative on a 3–4 week rotation to combat frequency fatigue. CTV campaigns are optimized on household reach and frequency; display and native on conversion-rate-weighted CPM.

06

Monthly Reporting & Cross-Channel Analysis

Monthly reports include: impression delivery by format and publisher, frequency distribution, viewability rates, click-through rate (display only), view-through conversion rate, cost-per-conversion by format, and cross-channel ROAS showing programmatic's contribution alongside paid search and paid social. We present findings in plain language with clear recommendations — not agency jargon and impression charts.

FAQ

Programmatic Advertising Questions Answered

What is programmatic advertising?
Programmatic advertising is the automated buying of digital ad inventory through real-time bidding (RTB) and private marketplace (PMP) deals. Instead of manually negotiating with publishers, programmatic platforms let you define your audience, budget, and bid parameters — then automatically buy the most relevant impressions across thousands of publishers simultaneously. This gives access to premium inventory at scale with precise targeting impossible through direct buys.
How is programmatic different from Google Display or Meta?
Google Display and Meta are walled gardens — you can only reach their users on their properties. Programmatic accesses the open web: news sites, premium publishers, niche content, streaming TV, podcasts, and digital out-of-home screens. This means reaching your audience everywhere they consume content. Programmatic also provides access to CTV/streaming inventory that Meta and Google don't offer, plus private marketplace deals with specific premium publishers at negotiated CPMs.
What budget do I need for programmatic advertising?
We recommend a minimum of $5,000/mo in programmatic ad spend for meaningful reach and frequency. CTV campaigns require $3,000+/mo to reach sufficient audience at effective frequency (3–5 exposures). Display and OLV campaigns can be meaningful at $2,000–3,000/mo for targeted niche audiences. Below $2,000/mo, programmatic rarely delivers enough impressions to build the frequency and attribution data needed for optimization.
How do you measure programmatic ROI?
We implement view-through conversion tracking (for CTV and display), cross-device attribution, and incrementality testing to isolate programmatic's true conversion contribution. We report cost-per-view-through-conversion alongside ROAS and CPA, segmented by format (CTV vs. display vs. video), audience segment, and publisher category. We don't accept impressions and reach as success metrics — every programmatic campaign has conversion accountability built in from day one.
What is CTV advertising and why is it growing?
Connected TV advertising delivers video ads to households watching streaming content on internet-connected TVs — Roku, Fire TV, Apple TV, and streaming apps like Hulu and Peacock. US CTV ad spend is forecast to exceed $35 billion in 2026, growing 14% year-over-year, as viewers shift from linear TV to streaming. CTV offers TV-quality creative impact with digital-precision audience targeting and measurable attribution — combining the best of television and digital channels.
