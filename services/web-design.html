CRO Design Process

Design Decisions Backed by Conversion Data

We don't make design decisions based on aesthetics or trends. Every layout choice, color decision, and CTA placement is made based on conversion research, heatmap data, and A/B test results from our portfolio.

01

Conversion Audit & Heatmap Analysis

We start by auditing your existing pages — analyzing heatmaps, session recordings, form abandonment data, and conversion funnel drop-off points. This tells us exactly where visitors lose interest or abandon, which informs every design decision before we sketch a single wireframe.

02

CRO-Informed Wireframing

Wireframes that map the psychological journey from first impression to conversion action. We establish hierarchy based on conversion priority, not brand aesthetics — the most important CTA gets the most prominent placement, above the fold, every time.

03

Design & Copy Development

Visual design that reinforces conversion signals: trust indicators (testimonials, logos, guarantees) placed at friction points, CTA copy tested for action-orientation, and form design minimized to only the fields that are genuinely necessary for your sales process.

04

Development with Speed as a Metric

We develop with Core Web Vitals as a hard requirement. Clean HTML/CSS, image optimization, lazy loading, and minimal third-party script loading. Target: LCP under 2.5 seconds, CLS under 0.1, FID under 100ms across all devices.

05

Live A/B Testing & CVR Improvement

Post-launch, we immediately begin A/B testing the highest-impact page elements. Tests run until statistical significance (minimum 95% confidence, minimum 100 conversions per variant). Monthly CVR improvement reports tied directly to ROAS impact across your paid channels.

FAQ

Web Design & CRO Questions Answered

What makes Vora's web design different from typical agencies?
We design with conversion rate as the primary metric, not aesthetics. Every design decision — headline placement, CTA color, form length, page structure, trust signal positioning — is made based on CRO research and A/B test results from our portfolio. We don't produce beautiful websites that don't convert; we produce pages that are both visually compelling and measurably effective at turning traffic into revenue.
Do you build landing pages specifically for paid traffic?
Yes — this is a core service and one of our most direct paths to ROAS improvement. We build dedicated landing pages for Google Ads, Meta Ads, and other paid channels — completely separate from your main website navigation. These pages have message-match to the specific ad creative, zero navigation distractions, and are A/B tested from launch day to continuously improve CVR. Average improvement vs. sending paid traffic to a homepage: 47% higher conversion rate.
How do you measure CRO success?
We measure conversion rate by page, revenue per visitor, form completion rate, checkout abandonment rate, and the resulting downstream impact on ROAS for all paid traffic channels. We connect every design change to revenue outcomes, not just engagement metrics like time-on-page or bounce rate, which are poor proxies for conversion performance.
What CMS or platform do you build on?
We build on WordPress, Webflow, Shopify, and custom HTML/CSS/JS depending on your requirements. For dedicated paid traffic landing pages, we often recommend Webflow or custom builds for maximum load speed and A/B testing flexibility. For eCommerce, Shopify with custom theme development is our standard. We recommend platforms based on your team's ability to manage content post-launch without ongoing developer dependency.
How long does a web design project take?
A single high-performance landing page takes 2–3 weeks from brief to live with A/B testing configured. A full website with 5–10 core pages takes 6–10 weeks. Larger eCommerce builds with custom functionality typically take 12–16 weeks. All timelines include conversion tracking setup, Core Web Vitals optimization, and initial A/B test configuration — not just design delivery.
