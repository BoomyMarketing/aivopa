Our Methodology

How We Build Retargeting Systems

Retargeting isn't set-and-forget remarketing. A properly built retargeting system segments audiences by purchase intent, sequences messaging across the consideration window, and controls cross-platform frequency so you reach — not annoy — your warm audience.

01

Audience Architecture & Intent Mapping

We map your website behavior to intent levels: high intent (product page viewers, cart abandoners, checkout abandoners), medium intent (category browsers, blog readers, 3+ minute site dwell), low intent (homepage bouncers, single-page visits). Each intent tier gets different creative messaging, different bid multipliers, and different frequency caps. High-intent audiences get aggressive retargeting with urgency creative and offer incentives. Low-intent audiences get lighter brand reinforcement creative at lower frequency. This prevents both under-serving your warmest prospects and over-saturating your coldest retargeting pool.

02

Pixel & Tracking Audit

Before building any retargeting campaign, we audit your current pixel and tracking implementation: Meta Pixel event coverage (ViewContent, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, Purchase), Google Tag firing, conversion window accuracy, and cross-device matching. Broken or incomplete pixels mean retargeting audiences are missing key segments — typically 30–50% of actual cart abandoners are invisible to platforms with poor pixel implementation. We fix tracking before we spend a dollar on retargeting.

03

Audience Segmentation & Exclusion Logic

Proper audience segmentation requires proper exclusion logic — buyers must be excluded from cart abandoner campaigns immediately. Recent purchasers should not see acquisition creative. Lapsed buyers need re-engagement creative, not new customer offers. We build custom audience segments for every campaign with proper exclusion chains, time-window rules (exclude purchases from last 30 days from prospecting; include 31–180 day buyers in win-back campaigns), and cross-platform suppression lists to prevent buyers from being retargeted across all channels simultaneously.

04

Creative Strategy by Audience Tier

Each audience segment needs creative designed for their specific stage in the consideration journey. Cart abandoners respond best to urgency (limited-time offer, social proof of scarcity, direct product reminder). Product page visitors respond to social proof (reviews, star ratings, before/after). Category browsers respond to education (why your brand, key differentiators). We develop creative briefs for each tier and produce 3 variants per segment for A/B testing — not one generic retargeting ad served to your entire warm audience.

05

Cross-Platform Frequency Management

Without cross-platform frequency control, a user might see your retargeting ad 3 times on Meta, 2 times on Google Display, and twice on TikTok in a single day — destroying brand perception. We set per-platform daily frequency caps (Meta: max 2/day, Google Display: max 3/day, TikTok: max 2/day), coordinate campaign timing to avoid simultaneous peak exposure, and monitor cross-platform reach overlap reports to ensure total frequency is optimal (3–7 exposures per week), not excessive.

06

Attribution & Incrementality

Retargeting attribution is inherently complex — users who see your retargeting ad might have converted anyway. We implement incrementality testing (holdout groups) to measure true lift from retargeting versus organic return rate. View-through and click-through conversion windows are set based on your purchase cycle (1-day view-through for impulse purchases, 7-day for mid-consideration, 30-day for high-ticket). Monthly reports show retargeting contribution to total revenue, cost-per-conversion by segment, and a clear incrementality estimate — so you know retargeting is driving real revenue, not just claiming credit for inevitable conversions.

FAQ

Retargeting Questions Answered

What is retargeting and how does it work?
Retargeting shows ads to people who visited your website or engaged with your brand but didn't convert. A pixel or tag identifies previous visitors — when they browse other sites, social platforms, or apps, your ads follow them. Retargeting audiences convert at 70% higher rates than cold audiences because they already have brand awareness and purchase intent.
How much of my ad budget should go to retargeting?
Accounts with sufficient audience size (2,000+ monthly website visitors) typically allocate 20–30% of total paid media budget to retargeting. For DTC ecommerce with cart abandonment, retargeting often drives 25–35% of total revenue at CPAs 40–60% lower than prospecting. For lead generation, retargeting typically accounts for 30–40% of total leads at 50–70% lower CPL. Below 1,000 monthly visitors, retargeting audiences are too thin for meaningful campaign performance.
What is RLSA and how does it improve Google Ads performance?
RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads) lets you adjust Google Search bids, messaging, and targeting for users who previously visited your site. When a past visitor searches your keyword, you can bid 50–200% higher (they're much more likely to convert), show different ad copy referencing their previous visit, or bid on broader keywords you'd exclude for cold traffic. RLSA consistently improves conversion rates by 2–3x on the same search queries.
How do you prevent ad fatigue in retargeting campaigns?
Ad fatigue in retargeting is caused by too-high frequency and single-message creative. We prevent fatigue through frequency caps (max 3 exposures per user per day across platforms), creative rotation (minimum 3 ad variants per segment, refreshed every 14 days), message sequencing (different message to cart abandoners vs. site visitors), and exclusion logic (buyers immediately excluded from retargeting and moved to upsell audiences).
Can you retarget across multiple platforms simultaneously?
Yes — and cross-platform retargeting is significantly more effective than single-platform remarketing. When a user sees your retargeting ad on Meta, then Google Display, then TikTok, cumulative exposure compounds purchase intent in a way single-platform frequency cannot replicate. We manage cross-platform retargeting as a unified strategy — controlling total frequency, sequencing messages so each platform plays a different role in the conversion path, and attributing revenue correctly across touch points.
