Email Services

Full-Stack Email Marketing for Performance Brands

We treat email as a revenue channel, not a brand channel. Every campaign, flow, and sequence is measured by revenue generated — not open rates or click rates in isolation.

⚡

Automation Flow Build

Welcome series, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back, and birthday flows — built and optimized in Klaviyo or HubSpot. These flows work 24/7 and typically generate 20–35% of total email revenue.

📧

Campaign Management

Weekly or bi-weekly broadcast campaigns segmented by purchase history, engagement tier, and product affinity. We write, design, and schedule every send — tracking revenue per campaign with attribution models that account for multi-touch journeys.

🎯

List Segmentation Strategy

Dynamic segments based on RFM scores (Recency, Frequency, Monetary value), engagement windows, product purchase patterns, and predicted LTV. The right message to the right segment reduces unsubscribe rates and increases revenue per send.

📊

Revenue Attribution Reporting

Email revenue attribution with proper attribution windows — distinguishing between flow-driven revenue and campaign-driven revenue, tracking CAC from email-acquired customers, and measuring email's contribution to blended ROAS.

📈

List Growth & Health

Opt-in strategy, pop-up optimization, lead magnet development, and list hygiene programs. We grow your list with high-intent subscribers and maintain deliverability above 98% through regular suppression management.

🔗

Email + Paid Media Integration

Sync email segments with Google Ads and Meta audiences. Suppress recent buyers from retargeting. Build lookalike audiences from high-LTV email segments. Email and paid media working together reduces blended CAC by 20–35%.

Welcome Series

FAQ

Email Marketing Questions Answered

What email platforms do you work with?
We specialize in Klaviyo for eCommerce brands and HubSpot for B2B and SaaS. We also work with Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, and Brevo. Platform recommendation depends on your business model, existing tech stack integrations, and the level of automation complexity and behavioral segmentation needed. We're platform-agnostic in recommendations — we use what performs best for your specific use case.
What email flows do you build first?
For most eCommerce brands, the highest-ROI flows to build in priority order are: abandoned cart (3-email sequence), welcome series, browse abandonment, post-purchase cross-sell, and win-back. These five flows alone typically generate 20–35% of total email revenue with minimal ongoing management effort once built correctly. We build all five within the first 30 days of engagement.
How do you grow an email list without damaging deliverability?
We use intent-based opt-in strategies — exit-intent popups with compelling lead magnets, post-purchase prompts, quiz and recommendation tools, and paid acquisition via Meta Lead Ads targeting demographics matching your buyer persona. We never use purchased lists. We maintain list health through regular suppression of subscribers inactive for 180+ days, protecting deliverability rates and inbox placement above 98%.
What open rates and revenue per email should I expect?
With proper list hygiene and segmentation, our clients typically achieve 35–50% open rates on automated flows and 18–28% on broadcast campaigns to engaged segments. Revenue per email varies significantly by industry — eCommerce clients average $0.08–$0.25 per send per subscriber on broadcast campaigns, and higher on triggered flows. We'll benchmark against your current performance during audit and set targets based on your list quality and engagement history.
How does email marketing complement paid ads?
Email reduces blended CAC by converting subscribers at zero incremental media cost after initial paid acquisition. For every repeat purchase driven by email after a paid-channel first purchase, your effective CAC on that customer drops significantly. Email also reduces paid retargeting costs by suppressing recent purchasers and engaged subscribers from expensive retargeting campaigns on Meta and Google — eliminating redundant spend on users already in your email nurture funnel.
