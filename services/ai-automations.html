FAQ

AI Marketing Automation Questions Answered

What marketing processes can you automate with AI?
We automate bid management, lead scoring, audience segmentation, CRM pipeline triggers, performance reporting, email campaign sequencing, ad creative rotation based on performance signals, and cross-channel attribution updates. Any marketing process that follows rules or requires data synthesis across platforms is a candidate for automation — we scope each engagement individually.
What tools and platforms do you integrate?
We integrate with Google Ads, Meta Ads, HubSpot, Salesforce, Klaviyo, GA4, Looker Studio, Shopify, WooCommerce, and most major marketing platforms via API. We build custom middleware solutions where native integrations don't exist or where performance requirements exceed what native connectors can handle.
How long does it take to build and deploy AI automations?
Simple automations — CRM triggers, email sequences, performance reporting dashboards — can be deployed within 1–2 weeks. Complex AI-driven systems like predictive lead scoring models, dynamic bid algorithms, or multi-platform data pipelines typically take 4–8 weeks from scoping through testing to production deployment.
Do I need technical staff to manage AI automations?
No. We build automations with non-technical marketing operators in mind. Every system includes documentation, a monitoring dashboard, alert thresholds, and manual override controls your marketing team can manage without developer involvement. We also provide ongoing support, monitoring, and quarterly optimization reviews included in all automation engagements.
How do AI automations reduce CAC?
AI automations reduce CAC through three primary mechanisms: (1) faster bid optimization responses — adjusting bids in real-time based on conversion probability signals rather than waiting for manual review cycles; (2) automated audience exclusions that eliminate wasted spend on unqualified segments as soon as performance data identifies them; and (3) automated creative rotation away from underperforming ads before they degrade ROAS significantly.
