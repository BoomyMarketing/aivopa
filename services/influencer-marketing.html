Influencer Services

What We Manage in Your Influencer Program

Influencer marketing is a full operational discipline — creator discovery, audience vetting, contract negotiation, brief development, content approval, posting monitoring, whitelisting setup, and performance attribution. We manage the entire workflow so you get campaigns that convert, not just posts that look good.

🔍

Creator Discovery & Audience Vetting

We identify creators using a combination of platform-native discovery, third-party tools (HypeAuditor, Modash, Upfluence), and vertical-specific research. Every potential creator is vetted for audience authenticity (fake follower %, bot engagement rate), engagement quality (comment depth vs. generic reactions), demographic alignment with your target customer, content-brand fit, and prior partnership history. We reject creators with inflated metrics before a single dollar is committed.

Rejects: fake followers, low engagement quality, poor brand fit
📋

Contract Negotiation & Rights

We handle all creator negotiations — usage rights (paid media, organic reposts, duration, geography), exclusivity periods (no competing brand posts for 30–60 days), deliverable specifications (format, length, required talking points, approval process), revision rounds, payment terms, and FTC disclosure requirements. We negotiate paid media whitelisting rights as standard — ensuring you can amplify every piece of creator content with paid spend, without additional licensing fees after delivery.

Full paid media rights negotiated in every contract
📝

Creative Briefing

Creator briefs are the single biggest differentiator between influencer campaigns that convert and campaigns that just generate impressions. We write performance briefs — required talking points, prohibited claims, hook structure recommendations, CTA requirements, brand guidelines, and visual references. Briefs are detailed enough to guide content quality without being prescriptive enough to kill authenticity. The goal is creator-owned content that happens to hit your conversion objectives, not scripted ads with a creator's face on them.

Brief quality drives 60% of content performance
✅

Content Approval & Compliance

All creator content goes through a structured approval process before publishing: compliance check (required disclosures present, prohibited claims absent, FTC #ad or #sponsored tag visible), message accuracy (product claims factually correct, no unauthorized health/safety statements), brand guideline adherence, and strategic review (does the hook lead? Is the CTA clear? Does it match the brief?). We manage revision requests with specific, actionable feedback — preserving creator authenticity while ensuring content meets your standards.

FTC disclosure compliance on every post
📡

Whitelisting & Paid Amplification

Whitelisting runs your paid ads from the creator's account rather than your brand account — the ad appears as the creator's content, not as a brand post. This dramatically improves authenticity perception and algorithmic favorability on TikTok and Meta. We identify top-performing organic influencer posts (highest engagement, best conversion rate from promo code data), secure whitelisting authorization from the creator, and run paid amplification targeting audiences similar to the creator's engaged followers — scaling the reach of what already works organically.

2.8x higher CVR vs. brand-account equivalent ads
📊

Attribution & Reporting

We build a multi-method attribution stack for influencer campaigns: unique promo codes per creator (direct purchase attribution), UTM-tagged bio links (session and conversion tracking in GA4), dedicated influencer landing pages (full funnel visibility), and post-campaign lift analysis (branded search volume, direct traffic). Monthly reports show cost-per-sale by creator, engagement-to-conversion rate, earned media value, and clear ROI. We don't hide behind "brand awareness" metrics — every campaign has conversion accountability.

Promo codes + UTMs + lift study = full attribution

Our Process

Influencer Campaign Methodology

Influencer campaigns fail when they're treated as one-off creative activations. We treat influencer as a channel — with systematic testing, performance measurement, and a roster of reliable creators who understand your brand and consistently deliver results.

01

Campaign Strategy & Creator Profile

We define your ideal creator profile: platform (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube — each reaches different demographics and content formats), vertical (which creator niches align with your customer's interests), audience demographics (age, gender, location, income indicators), content style (educational, entertainment, aspirational, authentic review), and follower range (nano/micro/macro tier based on budget and objective). We also define the campaign objective upfront — direct sales with promo codes, audience building, or a hybrid — because the objective shapes every downstream decision.

02

Creator Discovery & Shortlist

We build a creator shortlist of 20–30 candidates per campaign — more than you'll activate, so you have a strong backup pool when first-choice creators are unavailable or fail vetting. Each candidate is evaluated with third-party analytics: authentic follower ratio (minimum 85% real), engagement rate vs. category benchmark, audience demographic match, previous brand partnerships (category conflicts, quality of past sponsored content), and content posting frequency (creators who post daily have better algorithmic reach than once-weekly posters). We present a curated shortlist with full metrics — you approve the final selection.

03

Outreach & Contracting

We handle all creator outreach with personalized pitches — not mass blast templates. Outreach explains why we selected that specific creator (their specific content resonated, their audience matches our target), what the campaign involves, and an initial rate inquiry. Contracts are issued digitally via DocuSign covering deliverables, approval timelines, posting windows, FTC compliance obligations, exclusivity, usage rights, and payment schedule. We typically negotiate rates 10–20% below creator initial asks by bundling deliverables and offering faster payment terms.

04

Product Seeding & Brief Delivery

Product ships to creators with a physical or digital brief package: the creative brief (key messages, required disclosures, what NOT to say), brand guidelines (logo usage, color references, tone), example reference content (good examples from other creators, competitor content to differentiate from), and the tracking setup (unique promo code, UTM-tagged link for bio). For complex products, we schedule a brief call with creators to walk through the product and answer questions — creators who understand what they're promoting produce dramatically better content.

05

Content Review & Approval

All content is submitted for review before publishing. Our review checklist: FTC disclosure present and visible (first 3 lines of caption or first 5 seconds of video), prohibited claims absent, promo code correct, link correct, hook starts with the strongest moment, brand mentioned within first 30 seconds of video, CTA clear and specific. We provide revision feedback within 24 hours — specific, actionable notes ("the hook at 0:05 is stronger than the opening — lead with that") rather than vague direction. Most content requires 0–1 revision rounds with properly briefed creators.

06

Post-Publishing Monitoring & Amplification

After publishing, we monitor performance for the first 72 hours — the critical window for platform algorithm distribution. Posts with strong early engagement signals (saves, shares, comments) are flagged for paid amplification. We request whitelisting authorization from high-performing creators and set up paid promotion targeting look-alike audiences to the creator's engaged followers. Underperforming posts are analyzed for root cause: brief miss, wrong creator audience, or hook failure — and learnings feed into the next campaign brief to compound improvement over time.

FAQ

Influencer Marketing Questions Answered

What size influencers should I work with?
Nano-influencers (1K–10K) have the highest engagement (5–10%) and lowest cost, but limited reach. Micro-influencers (10K–100K) are the sweet spot for DTC brands — strong engagement (3–6%), niche authority, and reasonable rates ($300–2,000/post). Macro-influencers (100K–1M) deliver significant reach but lower engagement (1–3%) and high rates ($2,000–20,000/post). We typically recommend a micro-heavy mix — 70% micro, 20% mid-tier, 10% macro — for brands targeting conversion over awareness.
How do you vet influencers for fake followers?
Fake follower detection looks beyond follower count to engagement quality signals: engagement rate vs. category benchmark, comment quality (generic comments from accounts with no profile photos indicate bots), follower growth spikes (sudden gains of 10,000+ in a week indicate purchased followers), audience authenticity scores from HypeAuditor or Modash, and geographic audience alignment. We run all potential partners through our vetting process before committing any budget.
What is influencer whitelisting and why does it matter?
Whitelisting runs paid ads from the influencer's social account rather than your brand account — the ad appears as the creator's content. Whitelisted ads consistently outperform brand-account ads because the algorithm treats creator-account content more favorably and users engage with it as organic creator content rather than an advertisement. We negotiate whitelisting rights in all influencer contracts and amplify top-performing organic posts with paid spend.
How do you measure influencer campaign ROI?
We use multiple attribution methods layered together: unique promo codes per creator (direct purchase attribution), UTM parameters for click and session tracking, dedicated landing pages for full funnel visibility, and post-campaign lift analysis (branded search volume, direct traffic shifts). Monthly reports show cost-per-sale by creator, engagement-to-conversion rate, and clear ROI. We don't hide behind reach and impression metrics — every campaign has conversion accountability.
What content rights do I own after an influencer campaign?
By default, influencers retain content ownership — you have only the rights explicitly granted in the contract. We negotiate content licensing as part of every influencer agreement: paid media usage rights (run the creator's content as paid ads), organic brand reposting rights, duration (typically 6–12 months), and geographic scope. Usage rights increase creator rates by 20–50% — we build this into budget planning from the start so rights negotiation doesn't create post-campaign surprises.
