Affiliate Services

What We Manage for Your Affiliate Program

A successful affiliate program requires more than signing up on a platform and waiting for publishers to find you. It requires proactive recruitment, relationship management, fraud monitoring, and creative support — the same operational discipline as any other performance channel.

🏗️

Program Setup & Platform Configuration

End-to-end affiliate program launch: platform selection (Impact, ShareASale, CJ, Rakuten, PartnerStack), tracking pixel and postback URL configuration, commission structure design, publisher terms and conditions, creative asset library (banners, text links, product feeds), and deep link setup. We configure fraud prevention settings, cookie window parameters, and attribution rules before the first publisher goes live. Getting the infrastructure right before launch prevents costly tracking errors and commission disputes later.

Timeline: 2–4 weeks from kickoff to live program
🤝

Publisher Recruitment & Outreach

We identify and recruit publishers who actually drive quality conversions in your vertical — review sites, comparison engines, deal and coupon sites, content bloggers, email newsletter publishers, and influencers with proven affiliate sales history. Outreach includes personalized program pitch emails, commission tier proposals tailored to each publisher's traffic quality, and onboarding support to get them promoting quickly. We target publishers with your audience, not just high-traffic sites with generic audiences.

Month 1: 20–50 active publishers recruited and onboarded
📈

Ongoing Program Management

Active program management includes: weekly performance reviews (top publishers by revenue, conversion rate, and average order value), commission optimization for top performers, co-op promotion planning (seasonal campaigns, exclusive coupon codes), publisher communication and relationship nurturing, creative refresh cycles, and competitive benchmarking against programs in your category. We act as your dedicated affiliate program manager — publishers get fast responses, exclusive opportunities, and the support they need to promote your brand effectively.

Monthly reporting, weekly publisher communication
🛡️

Fraud Prevention & Compliance

Affiliate fraud costs US advertisers $1.4B+ annually. We prevent it through: real-time transaction monitoring for suspicious conversion velocity, IP and device fingerprinting for lead quality validation, click-to-conversion ratio analysis to flag cookie stuffing, coupon code auditing to identify unauthorized usage, branded keyword trademark monitoring, and platform-level fraud detection tools. Fraudulent publishers are terminated, transactions are reversed, and your commission budget goes only to real, incremental sales.

Zero tolerance for fraud — every suspicious transaction reviewed
💰

Commission Structure Optimization

Commission structures determine which publishers promote you and how aggressively. We model program economics — your margin, LTV, and CAC target — to design commission rates that attract quality publishers and remain profitable at scale. Structures we design: flat CPA (fixed dollar per conversion), revenue share (% of sale), tiered commissions (higher rates for higher-volume publishers), performance bonuses (seasonal escalators), and hybrid models for SaaS (first-month flat + recurring monthly commission per retained customer).

Commissions modeled against LTV — not just first-order margin
📊

Attribution & Analytics

Affiliate attribution requires rigorous deduplication — when a customer clicks an affiliate link, then a paid search ad, then converts, who gets credit? We configure data-driven attribution models that give affiliates appropriate credit without over-paying for last-click assists that would have converted anyway. Monthly reporting covers: revenue by publisher, average order value by publisher type, new vs. returning customer split, incrementality estimates, and full P&L showing affiliate ROAS net of commissions and platform fees.

Monthly P&L reporting — revenue, commissions, net margin by publisher

Our Process

Affiliate Program Launch Methodology

Launching an affiliate program is a 90-day project with distinct phases. Getting the foundation right — tracking, commission economics, fraud controls — determines whether you build a profitable revenue channel or an expensive liability.

01

Program Economics Audit

Before building anything, we model your program economics: product margin (gross margin net of COGS), customer LTV (average repeat purchase rate and value), current blended CAC from paid channels, and target affiliate ROAS. These numbers determine the maximum sustainable commission rate — too low and quality publishers won't promote you; too high and the program isn't profitable. We also benchmark commission rates against your top 5 competitors on the platform to ensure you're competitive without overpaying.

02

Platform Selection & Technical Setup

Platform choice depends on vertical coverage, publisher network overlap, and technical requirements. We select and configure the platform, integrate tracking pixels with your ecommerce platform (Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento, custom) or CRM (for B2B lead gen programs), set up postback URLs for server-side tracking, configure deduplication logic with your other channels, and test the full conversion tracking path end-to-end before going live. Broken tracking is the most common affiliate program failure — we prevent it by testing every attribution scenario before launch.

03

Publisher Recruitment Strategy

We identify your ideal publisher profile based on your product category, audience demographics, and conversion funnel. For DTC ecommerce: deal/coupon sites, cashback portals, product review blogs, and gift guide publishers. For SaaS: software review platforms (G2, Capterra), tech bloggers, and email newsletter publishers in your vertical. For B2B lead gen: industry association sites, professional LinkedIn newsletter publishers, and B2B content hubs. We create a tiered recruitment target list — top 20 priority publishers, 50 secondary targets — and execute personalized outreach with compelling program pitch decks.

04

Publisher Onboarding & Asset Delivery

Approved publishers need immediate access to everything required to promote your brand: high-quality banner ads in all standard sizes (300x250, 728x90, 160x600, 320x50), text link copy, product feed for comparison engines, deep link generation tools, and promotional guidelines. We create a publisher welcome kit with brand guidelines, content restrictions (what affiliates can and cannot say), exclusive offer calendar, and dedicated contact for program questions. Publishers who get immediate, high-quality onboarding start promoting faster and generate revenue sooner.

05

Fraud Monitoring & Publisher Quality Control

The first 30 days after launch are high-risk for fraud as non-vetted publishers test the program. We implement week-1 monitoring: review every conversion for suspicious patterns (unusually high conversion rate from a new publisher, conversions clustering at unusual hours, order values inconsistent with publisher's audience), audit coupon code usage, and investigate any publisher generating sales without corresponding click volume. High-risk publishers are put on manual approval for commissions until quality is verified. A clean program from day one protects your budget and your brand.

06

Scale & Optimization

Months 3–6 focus on scaling what works: increasing commission tiers for consistently high-performing publishers, launching exclusive seasonal promotions (Black Friday custom codes, product launch previews, VIP publisher early access), identifying partnership opportunities with your top publishers (co-marketed content, dedicated email campaigns), and expanding into new publisher categories based on which types delivered the best new customer quality (measured by post-purchase LTV, not just first-order value). The goal is a self-reinforcing flywheel — the more revenue your program generates, the more attractive it is to premium publishers.

FAQ

Affiliate Marketing Questions Answered

What does an affiliate marketing agency do?
An affiliate marketing agency builds, manages, and grows your affiliate program — the system where external publishers (bloggers, review sites, coupon sites, influencers) promote your products and earn a commission for each sale or lead they generate. The agency handles platform setup, publisher recruitment, commission structure design, creative asset management, fraud monitoring, performance analysis, and publisher relationship management. The goal is a network of partners generating incremental revenue on a pure pay-for-performance basis.
What affiliate platforms do you work with?
We work across all major affiliate platforms: Impact, ShareASale, CJ Affiliate, Rakuten Advertising, Awin, and PartnerStack for SaaS and B2B programs. Platform selection depends on your vertical, existing publisher relationships in the network, technical requirements, and budget. Impact and CJ Affiliate have the broadest publisher reach for ecommerce and DTC. PartnerStack is purpose-built for SaaS partner programs. We recommend the platform with the largest publisher overlap for your specific product category.
How do you prevent affiliate fraud?
Affiliate fraud takes multiple forms: cookie stuffing, fake leads, forced clicks, and trademark bidding. We prevent it through real-time transaction monitoring for suspicious patterns, publisher vetting before approval, click-to-conversion ratio analysis, IP and device fingerprinting for lead quality, coupon and promo code audits, and platform-level fraud detection tools. Fraudulent publishers are terminated and chargebacks issued — your commission budget goes only to real, incremental sales.
What commission structure should I use?
Commission structure depends on your margin, customer LTV, and the type of publishers you want to attract. Flat-rate CPA is simplest (10–30% of first order value for ecommerce). Revenue share is preferred by publishers wanting long-term earning potential. Tiered commissions incentivize top performers. For SaaS and subscriptions, recurring commissions attract serious affiliate partners. We model your program economics — margin, LTV, CAC target — to design a structure that's attractive to publishers and profitable for you.
How long does it take to build an affiliate program?
Setup (platform configuration, tracking, creative assets, terms): 2–4 weeks. Initial publisher recruitment and onboarding: 30–60 days for 20–50 active affiliates. Revenue ramp: most programs reach meaningful revenue ($10k+/mo) within 90–120 days if the product has proven demand and competitive commissions. The key constraint is usually publisher recruitment — quality publishers prioritize programs with strong brand recognition, competitive commissions, and responsive program managers.
