Ad Formats

LinkedIn Ad Formats We Manage

LinkedIn's advertising formats serve different stages of the B2B buying journey — from awareness with decision-makers to direct lead capture to multi-stakeholder ABM programs. We select and combine formats based on your deal size, sales cycle length, and ICP definition.

📰

Sponsored Content

Native ads in the LinkedIn feed — single image, video, carousel, and document ads. The primary format for building awareness and driving consideration with ICP audiences. We optimize ad creative around LinkedIn's unique "professional scroll" behavior: educational hooks, specific-outcome headlines, and credibility-forward body copy.

Best for: Top and mid-funnel — thought leadership, offer introduction, lead nurture
📋

Lead Gen Forms

In-platform forms that pre-populate with the user's LinkedIn profile data (name, title, company, email). Removes landing page friction entirely — conversion rates are typically 3–5x higher than equivalent landing page campaigns. We design form questions to qualify leads at the point of capture, not after.

Typical CVR: 8–13% vs. 2–4% on landing page campaigns
🏢

Account-Based Marketing (ABM)

Upload a target company list and run coordinated ad sequences to specific job functions within those accounts. We build ABM programs with role-based creative sequences: different messaging for economic buyers, technical evaluators, and end users. Multi-stakeholder penetration dramatically improves deal close rates.

Best for: Enterprise deals $50K+ ACV, named account targeting programs
✉️

Message Ads & InMail

Direct messages delivered to targeted LinkedIn inboxes — only when recipients are active on LinkedIn. We write high-converting InMail copy focused on a single, specific offer (audit, assessment, demo, resource) relevant to the recipient's role. InMail works best as a bottom-funnel tactic for warm audiences who've already engaged with your Sponsored Content.

Best for: Bottom-funnel warm audiences, high-value offer delivery
💬

Conversation Ads

Interactive message ads that let recipients choose their own path through multiple-choice responses — qualifying themselves into the right offer based on their answers. We design conversation flows that route different buyer personas to the most relevant landing page, resource, or lead form, increasing relevance and conversion rate simultaneously.

Best for: Multi-persona targeting, content personalization at scale
🎯

Retargeting & Matched Audiences

Re-engage website visitors, video viewers, Lead Gen Form openers, and CRM contacts with sequential content. We build LinkedIn retargeting sequences that move warm audiences through the funnel: awareness content → consideration offer → demo/consultation request. CRM matching allows us to target your existing pipeline with coordinated nurture campaigns.

Typical pipeline conversion lift: 40–60% on CRM-matched sequences

Our Methodology

How We Generate Qualified B2B Pipeline on LinkedIn

Most LinkedIn campaigns underperform because agencies treat them like Meta campaigns with different creative. LinkedIn requires a fundamentally different approach: audience precision over volume, educational content over promotional copy, and pipeline attribution over click-through metrics.

01

ICP Definition & Audience Architecture

We build your Ideal Customer Profile in LinkedIn targeting terms: job title exclusion/inclusion lists (more precise than LinkedIn's generic job function categories), seniority levels, company size ranges, industry segments, and geographic filters. Most LinkedIn advertisers use LinkedIn's broad job function targeting — we build custom title lists of 200–500 specific job titles that match your actual buyers, dramatically improving lead quality at the same CPM.

02

CRM Integration & Pipeline Attribution

We connect LinkedIn's Insight Tag with your CRM to track leads from first LinkedIn touch to closed deal. This requires mapping LinkedIn campaign IDs to CRM lead sources and configuring appropriate attribution windows (typically 30-day click, 7-day view for B2B). Without this integration, you're optimizing toward form fills — not qualified pipeline. We build the full attribution chain before launching campaigns.

03

Creative Strategy by Funnel Stage

Top-of-funnel: educational thought leadership content (data insights, industry benchmarks, "X mistakes" format) that adds value without asking for anything — building trust and authority with ICP decision-makers. Mid-funnel: specific outcome content (case studies, ROI calculators, comparison guides) for audiences who've engaged with top-funnel. Bottom-funnel: direct response offers (free audit, 30-min consultation, demo) with specific benefit statements and clear CTA. Each stage gets different creative, different targeting, and different success metrics.

04

Lead Qualification at the Form Level

We design Lead Gen Forms with 3–5 qualifying questions that simultaneously collect lead data and qualify intent: "What's your current monthly ad spend?", "What's your primary acquisition channel?", "How soon are you looking to hire an agency?" These questions disqualify unqualified leads before they enter your CRM, reducing sales team time wasted on bad fits and improving the CPL-to-SQL conversion rate for your sales team.

05

Budget Allocation & Bid Optimization

LinkedIn's Campaign Manager defaults to Maximum Delivery bidding, which burns budget on low-quality impressions. We use Manual CPC bidding for most campaigns — paying only when users click with intent, not for passive impressions. Budget is allocated by funnel stage (typically 60% prospecting / 40% retargeting) and adjusted monthly based on cost-per-SQL data from CRM attribution, not campaign-level click metrics.

06

Pipeline Reporting & Continuous Optimization

Monthly reporting covers: cost-per-lead by campaign, lead-to-SQL conversion rate by audience segment, cost-per-SQL, pipeline generated and closed (with appropriate attribution lag), and creative performance by format and message. We flag underperforming audience segments early and shift budget to the ICP sub-segments generating the most pipeline — not just the most leads.

FAQ

LinkedIn Ads Questions Answered

How much does LinkedIn advertising cost in New York?
LinkedIn Ads management at Vora starts at $1,500/mo. LinkedIn has a minimum daily spend of $10/campaign. We recommend a minimum LinkedIn ad budget of $4,000/mo — LinkedIn CPCs are higher than Google or Meta ($5–$15 per click vs. $1–$3) but reach B2B decision-makers that no other platform can match at scale. We optimize for cost-per-qualified-lead, not cost-per-click, which is why the higher CPC is economically justified for most B2B companies.
Why are LinkedIn Ads more expensive than other platforms?
LinkedIn CPCs and CPMs are 3–5x higher than Meta or Google because you're buying access to professional audiences by job title, seniority, industry, and company size — data no other platform has at this precision. A $12 click from a VP of Engineering at a 500-person SaaS company is worth more than a $1 click from an unqualified Google searcher. LinkedIn's value is in lead quality, not volume. We track cost-per-pipeline-opportunity to prove this ROI concretely.
What B2B companies benefit most from LinkedIn Ads?
LinkedIn delivers the strongest ROI for B2B companies with: average deal sizes above $10,000 (so higher CPCs are justified), decision-making cycles of 30+ days (where nurture sequences build value), and target audiences definable by job title, industry, or company size. SaaS, professional services, financial services, recruitment, enterprise software, and management consulting consistently see strong pipeline from LinkedIn advertising.
What is Account-Based Marketing on LinkedIn?
ABM on LinkedIn lets you upload a target company list and run coordinated ad campaigns to employees at those specific companies. We build ABM programs that sequence content by job function — different ads for the economic buyer (CFO/VP Finance) vs. the technical evaluator (IT/Engineering) vs. the end user (Sales/Marketing). This coordinated multi-stakeholder approach dramatically improves deal close rates vs. generic LinkedIn campaigns targeting broad audience segments.
How do you measure LinkedIn Ads ROI?
We connect LinkedIn's Insight Tag with your CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, or equivalent) to track leads from first LinkedIn touch through to closed deal. Our reporting shows cost-per-lead, cost-per-SQL (Sales Qualified Lead), cost-per-opportunity, and pipeline ROI — not just LinkedIn's native platform metrics. This CRM-connected attribution proves LinkedIn's actual contribution to revenue, not just form fills that may never become customers.
