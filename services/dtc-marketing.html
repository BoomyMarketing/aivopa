How We Grow DTC Brands

DTC growth requires more than running Meta ads. The brands that scale sustainably build systems: a creative testing program that keeps CPMs from rising, a retention engine that drives repeat revenue without incremental ad spend, and unit economics discipline that prevents scaling unprofitable acquisition. We build all three.

Unit Economics & CAC Modeling

DTC brands fail by optimizing for channel ROAS instead of blended nCAC against LTV. We model your unit economics before setting any campaign targets: gross margin analysis, LTV curve by acquisition cohort (Month-2, Month-6, Month-12 repeat purchase rates), contribution margin by product and channel, and payback period calculation. From this model we derive your sustainable nCAC (new customer acquisition cost) — the maximum you can spend to acquire a customer while remaining profitable within your payback window. All campaign ROAS targets are set against this nCAC model, not industry averages.

Our Process

DTC Growth Framework

We approach DTC accounts with a sequential framework: model the unit economics, fix the conversion foundation, build the creative engine, then scale acquisition — in that order. Scaling before fixing the foundation is the most common reason DTC brands burn budget without profitable growth.

01

Unit Economics Audit

We start by modeling your DTC unit economics: gross margin by product (what percentage of revenue is contribution after COGS?), customer lifetime value by cohort (how much does a customer acquired on Meta actually spend over 12 months?), current blended nCAC (what are you actually paying to acquire new customers when you divide total ad spend by new customer count?), and payback period (how many months until CAC is recovered from gross profit on repeat purchases?). This audit often reveals that DTC brands are running campaigns that appear profitable on channel ROAS but are actually acquiring customers below sustainable margin — and that certain products or audience segments have dramatically better unit economics than others.

02

Conversion Foundation Audit

Before scaling acquisition spend, we audit the conversion foundation: tracking accuracy (Meta CAPI event match quality, GA4 purchase event accuracy vs. Shopify orders), product page CVR (are landing pages losing 80%+ of paid traffic before add-to-cart?), checkout completion rate (what % of initiated checkouts result in purchase?), and email flow coverage (are cart abandoners being recovered via both email and SMS?). DTC brands spending on acquisition while losing 4% of revenue to checkout abandonment (industry average) and missing cart recovery flows are paying for traffic they're giving away. We fix the funnel before increasing the flow into it.

03

Creative Engine Build

We establish a sustainable creative testing program: a monthly creative calendar with 4–6 concept directions per month (each testing a different message angle — transformation, social proof, problem-first, comparison, offer-led), weekly production cadence delivering 3–5 new UGC assets per week, and a creative performance scorecard tracking CPP (cost per purchase), hook retention rate, and thumb-stop rate by concept. The creative engine runs independently of campaign management — creative production doesn't pause between test cycles, ensuring there's always fresh creative ready to replace fatiguing ads before performance drops.

04

Campaign Launch & Scaling Protocol

DTC campaigns launch in a phased scaling protocol: first $5K/mo validates creative and audience hypotheses, $5K–25K/mo scales proven concepts to broader audiences, $25K–100K/mo introduces new channels (Google Shopping, YouTube, programmatic retargeting), $100K+/mo diversifies into influencer/creator partnerships and international markets via Shopify Markets. Each scaling threshold requires validated unit economics before proceeding — we don't advance to the next tier until the current tier's nCAC is below the LTV-derived sustainability threshold. This prevents the common DTC failure mode: scaling fast to a revenue target while the economics get worse with every dollar of additional spend.

05

Retention Program Activation

In parallel with acquisition scaling, we build the retention program that makes growth sustainable: Klaviyo email flows (all core DTC flows live within 30 days of onboarding), SMS campaigns for high-urgency recovery (abandoned cart SMS typically recovers an additional 8–12% of abandoners beyond email), subscription program launch (for consumable products — converting one-time buyers to auto-replenish subscribers captures 3–5x LTV at near-zero incremental CAC), and loyalty tier structure (rewarding repeat buyers with escalating benefits that increase purchase frequency and referral behavior). Retention investment pays compounding returns — every percentage point increase in Month-2 repeat purchase rate translates to significant LTV improvement across all future acquisition cohorts.

06

Monthly Growth Review

Monthly DTC reviews cover the full picture: blended ROAS vs. target and trend, nCAC by channel and product, cohort LTV analysis (Month-2 and Month-6 repeat purchase rates for each acquisition cohort), email and SMS revenue contribution, subscription and LTV trend, creative performance leaderboard (which concepts drove the most efficient new customer acquisition), and a forward-looking growth model (what nCAC and LTV combination allows us to increase spend by 25–50% next month while maintaining target payback period). Every growth decision is rooted in economics — not gut feel about which ads look good.

FAQ

DTC Marketing Questions Answered

What makes DTC marketing different from general ecommerce marketing?
DTC (direct-to-consumer) marketing has unique characteristics: the brand owns the entire customer relationship (no Amazon intermediary), meaning full data ownership, direct email/SMS communication, and owned audience building. DTC economics require sustainable unit economics — customer acquisition cost (CAC) must be justified by customer lifetime value (LTV), not just first-order ROAS. A DTC brand acquiring customers at 1x LTV is unprofitable even with strong channel ROAS. We model LTV-adjusted CAC targets before setting campaign ROAS floors.
How do you calculate the right CAC for a DTC brand?
The right new customer acquisition cost (nCAC) target is derived from your product's gross margin and LTV, not from competitive benchmarking. Formula: sustainable nCAC = (LTV × gross margin %) × payback period tolerance. For a brand with $150 LTV, 60% gross margin, and 6-month payback tolerance: sustainable nCAC = ($150 × 60%) × (6/12) = $45. We model your specific LTV curve (Month-1 through Month-12 repeat purchase rates) before setting nCAC targets for campaign optimization.
What creative works best for DTC paid social?
DTC paid social is dominated by UGC-style video content — authentic, first-person testimonials and product demos that match native content on TikTok and Instagram Reels. Highest-performing DTC creative formats: transformation stories (before/after with specific results), problem-first hooks, social proof compilations (review screenshots, rating graphs), unboxing and first-use demos, and comparison creative. We produce 3–5 new UGC concepts per week for active DTC accounts — creative velocity is the single biggest differentiator between brands that scale and those that plateau.
What retention strategies should a DTC brand prioritize?
Priority retention strategies: Klaviyo email flows (post-purchase sequence, win-back campaigns, VIP loyalty), SMS for high-urgency re-engagement at 98% open rates, subscription or auto-replenish programs (converting one-time buyers dramatically increases LTV and revenue predictability), and loyalty programs (points-based or tiered rewards that incentivize repeat purchase and referral). We build retention metrics into monthly reporting — Month-2 repeat purchase rate and Month-6 cohort LTV — so you see retention trends before they compound into a CAC sustainability problem.
When should a DTC brand expand from Meta/TikTok to other channels?
Expand from Meta/TikTok when: frequency rises above 3.5 for your core prospecting audience (indicating saturation), CPMs are rising faster than conversion rate improvements can offset, or you've exhausted primary creative angles. Logical DTC expansion sequence: (1) Google Shopping (captures in-market buyers), (2) YouTube TrueView (video product demos for higher-AOV products), (3) Programmatic retargeting (extending reach beyond Meta/Google), (4) Influencer/UGC amplification. Each channel is added only after the prior channel's economics are proven.
