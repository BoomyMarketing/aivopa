Measuring What Matters: ROAS, CAC & LTV for Content Marketing

The three performance metrics that Vora tracks for every content marketing engagement:

  • ROAS (Return on Ad Spend): Revenue generated per dollar of media spend. Vora targets 3-5x ROAS for paid channels, with SEO channels delivering 8-15x ROAS over 12-month horizons as organic traffic scales without proportional cost increase.
  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Total marketing cost divided by new customers acquired. Vora benchmarks your CAC against industry standards and works systematically to reduce it through audience refinement, conversion optimization, and channel mix improvement.
  • LTV (Lifetime Value): Total revenue a customer generates over their relationship with your business. Understanding LTV allows Vora to justify higher CAC for high-value customer segments — unlocking market share that conservative CAC targets leave on the table.

The Vora Revenue Formula

Every campaign we run is evaluated against a simple formula: (LTV × Conversion Rate) ÷ CAC = ROAS justification threshold. When ROAS exceeds the justification threshold, we scale. When it falls below, we diagnose and optimize before scaling further. This discipline prevents the budget waste that characterizes most digital marketing engagements.
