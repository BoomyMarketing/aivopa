Platform Digital Marketing: Choosing Channels by ROAS, Not Popularity

Platform digital marketing — allocating ad spend across Google, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, and programmatic — requires channel selection based on ROAS data, not industry convention. Vora's data-driven platform allocation framework from $50M+ in managed spend.

Performance Summary

Platform digital marketing ROAS varies significantly by channel, business type, and audience. Google Search delivers the highest intent-capture ROAS; Meta delivers the best demand-creation ROAS; LinkedIn delivers the highest LTV-adjusted B2B ROAS. Allocation should match each platform's strength to your funnel stage and customer economics.

Platform Digital Marketing: The ROAS-First Selection Framework

Most platform digital marketing allocation decisions are made based on familiarity ("everyone uses Google Ads"), industry convention ("B2B companies should be on LinkedIn"), or media consumption ("TikTok is growing fast"). None of these is a ROAS argument. Vora's platform allocation framework starts from business economics: what is the CAC target for each customer segment, what is the LTV that justifies that CAC, and which platform can reach the right audience at a cost per impression/click that makes the CAC target achievable?

This economics-first approach sometimes produces counterintuitive recommendations: a B2B company with $50,000 ACV may be better served by Google Search + organic SEO than LinkedIn despite LinkedIn's excellent B2B targeting, because the cost-per-qualified-lead on LinkedIn ($150-$400) may exceed the $80 target CAC that a $50,000 ACV product's economics justify at 5:1 LTV:CAC. The platform's targeting capability is irrelevant if the economics don't work at that platform's CPMs and conversion rates.

Platform-Specific ROAS Optimization Levers

Each platform has platform-specific optimization levers that have the highest ROAS impact. Google Search: negative keyword expansion (reduces wasted spend by 20-30%), Quality Score improvement (reduces CPC by 15-20% per point), and Smart Bidding with sufficient conversion data. Meta: Creative variety and rotation frequency (top 20% of creative combinations produce 80% of conversion value), CAPI implementation for signal quality, and Advantage+ campaign structure. LinkedIn: lead gen form ads (50-70% lower CPA than landing page ads), document ads for high-value B2B audiences, and conversation ads for ABM campaigns. Google Shopping: feed optimization (title, attribute, and price accuracy), bidding by product margin rather than uniform ROAS targets, and smart shopping conversion value tracking.

Platform Budget Allocation: The Dynamic Approach

Most platform digital marketing programs allocate budgets on quarterly cycles — a decision made in January determines platform allocation through March. Vora's approach: weekly budget review and reallocation based on real-time ROAS data. When Google Search campaigns are consistently hitting 5x+ ROAS but Meta is delivering 2.8x, budget should move toward Google Search. When a Meta campaign enters the learning phase and temporarily underperforms, budget shouldn't be reduced — that penalizes the algorithm's data collection process. Distinguishing between temporary optimization dips and sustained underperformance requires data discipline that quarterly reviews can't provide.

The Platform Saturation Problem

Every advertising platform has a saturation point where additional spend generates diminishing marginal ROAS. Vora monitors saturation signals — rising CPAs, declining conversion rates, search impression share approaching 80-90% — that indicate a platform is nearing its scalable capacity at current strategy. When saturation is detected, Vora redirects incremental budget to under-invested platforms rather than forcing scale beyond the efficiency cliff. This platform portfolio management consistently outperforms single-platform concentration at scale.

Cross-Platform Attribution: The Critical Missing Layer

Multi-platform digital marketing without unified attribution produces systematically misleading ROAS data: each platform overcounts its own contribution to conversions influenced by multiple channels. A customer who saw a Meta video ad, clicked a Google Search ad, and converted through a retargeting ad will be counted as a conversion by all three platforms. Without a unified attribution layer, businesses believe they're generating 3x the revenue they actually are. Vora implements GA4 as the authoritative conversion source, with platform-level reporting used for optimization signal data rather than revenue measurement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which platform delivers the best digital marketing ROAS?

Google Shopping for e-commerce (avg 5.8x), Google Search for service businesses (avg 4.1x), Meta for DTC consumer products (avg 3.6x). Platform ROAS depends heavily on creative quality, audience architecture, and attribution implementation — these averages assume optimized programs.

How should I allocate budget across digital marketing platforms?

Allocate first to the platform with strongest ROAS data for your business type. Scale on that platform until saturation signals appear. Then diversify to the second-highest-ROAS platform for your audience. Weekly budget review allows continuous reallocation as relative platform performance shifts.

How do I measure ROAS across multiple platforms accurately?

Use GA4 or a third-party attribution tool (Triple Whale, Northbeam) as the authoritative conversion source — not individual platform dashboards. Each platform overreports its own contribution. The unified attribution layer shows true blended ROAS after correcting for multi-platform double-counting.

When should I add a new digital marketing platform?

When your primary platform is approaching saturation (90%+ impression share, rising CPAs), when new audience segments are identified that your primary platform doesn't reach effectively, or when a new platform launches targeting capabilities that align with your highest-value customer profiles.

Does Vora manage multi-platform digital marketing?

Yes. Vora manages campaigns across Google Ads, Meta Ads, TikTok, LinkedIn, and programmatic — all on a unified attribution platform. Budget is reallocated weekly based on real-time ROAS data, maximizing blended returns across the full platform mix.
