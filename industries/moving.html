Our Process

How We Fill Your Moving Calendar in 48 Hours

1
Service Area & Move Type Audit

Define your service areas (boroughs, counties, long-distance corridors), move types (residential, commercial, specialty), and minimum job sizes. This shapes geotargeting, keyword themes, and landing page offers.

2
LSA Setup & Verification

Navigate the Google LSA background check and license verification process. Set up your LSA profile with service categories, photos, hours, and response settings. LSA leads start arriving within 48–72 hours of approval.

3
Google Search Campaign Build

Build campaigns segmented by move type and geography. Create landing pages with instant quote CTAs, trust signals (insurance, reviews, years in business), and call tracking numbers. Launch with tightly controlled initial budgets.

4
Review Generation Setup

Implement post-move SMS/email review request sequence. Train dispatch team on optimal timing (24–48 hrs after job completion) and scripts. Target 5+ new reviews per week during peak season to build review velocity ahead of competitors.

5
Peak Season Scaling

Scale Google Search and LSA budgets starting May 1. Add ad scheduling for peak booking hours (evenings and weekends). Run month-end campaigns targeting lease-turnover moves. Adjust service area targeting to avoid zip codes beyond profitable radius during peak demand.

6
Job Tracking & ROI Reporting

Monthly reporting on leads by channel, quotes given, jobs booked, and revenue generated. Track which campaigns produce the highest-value jobs (long-distance, commercial, specialty) to optimize budget allocation toward maximum gross profit per dollar spent.

Google Search is the primary channel — people searching 'movers NYC,' 'moving company Brooklyn,' or 'long distance movers New York' are actively planning a move and ready to get quotes. Local Services Ads (LSA) appear above standard Google Ads and work on a pay-per-lead basis, making them highly efficient for moving companies. Local SEO and Google Maps ranking captures neighborhood-level searches. Reviews on Google are critical — most customers compare at least 3 companies before booking.

May through September is peak moving season in NYC — particularly May, June, and the last weekend of each month when leases turn over. We scale budgets significantly during peak season and tighten targeting to priority service areas. October through April is off-peak but still active for long-distance and commercial moves. We maintain baseline campaigns year-round and use off-peak months to build Local SEO and review volume in preparation for the next peak season.
