Our Process

How We Grow Automotive Businesses

Automotive has one of the longest online research journeys of any consumer category. We build presence across every touchpoint — from first search to showroom visit.

1

Inventory Feed Setup

Connect your DMS (Dealer Management System) to Google Merchant Center for Vehicle Inventory Ads and to Meta's catalog for Automotive Inventory Ads. Feed must update daily — stale inventory listings waste budget on sold vehicles.

2

Campaign Architecture

Separate campaigns for new vehicles, used vehicles, certified pre-owned, service department, and conquest. Each has different CPCs, conversion rates, and margin profiles — mixed campaigns obscure performance and waste budget.

3

GBP & Local SEO

Optimize your Google Business Profile with accurate hours, service department, 50+ photos, and structured service offerings. Establish a review request cadence — auto repair shops with 100+ Google reviews see 40% more Maps calls than those with fewer than 20.

4

Call Tracking & Lead Routing

CallRail tracking numbers per campaign, call recording for quality assurance, and lead routing rules to route service calls separately from sales inquiries. Lead volume without lead quality context is useless data.

5

Retargeting Setup

VDP retargeting for shoppers who viewed specific vehicles. Service page retargeting for owners who visited but didn't book. Conquest audiences targeting competitor brand and model searches. All retargeting excluded from prospecting campaigns to avoid budget overlap.

6

CRM Integration & Monthly Reporting

Connect ad platform leads to your DMS or CRM. Monthly review of cost-per-sold-unit by campaign. Adjust budget allocation based on which channels produce the lowest cost-per-sale — not lowest cost-per-click.

FAQ

Automotive Marketing Questions

What automotive businesses do you work with?

New and used car dealerships, independent auto repair shops, tire and service centers, auto detailing, car rental companies, EV dealerships, and automotive aftermarket brands. We tailor strategy to your revenue model — whether you're selling vehicles, service appointments, or parts.

How do Google Vehicle Inventory Ads work for dealerships?

Google Vehicle Inventory Ads (VIA) pull your live inventory feed and show individual vehicle listings (with photo, make, model, year, price, and mileage) directly in search results. Shoppers searching for a specific make and model see your matching inventory above organic results. We set up and maintain the inventory feed, campaign structure, and bidding strategy.

How does Meta advertising work for car dealerships?

Meta's Automotive Inventory Ads (AIA) work like dynamic product ads for vehicles — showing relevant inventory to users based on their browsing behavior and stated vehicle interests. We also run lead generation campaigns with Meta Lead Forms for test drive requests and trade-in valuations, reducing friction vs. sending traffic to a website form.

What metrics do you optimize for in automotive campaigns?

For dealerships: cost-per-vehicle-detail-page-view, cost-per-lead (form fill or call), and showroom visits. For service departments: cost-per-appointment. For repair shops: inbound calls and booked appointments. We track through to CRM (DealerSocket, VinSolutions, or CDK) to connect ad spend to actual vehicle sales and service revenue.

How long before a dealership sees lead volume from paid search?

Google Search and Vehicle Inventory Ads generate leads within 48–72 hours of launch. Lead volume stabilizes within 30 days as Quality Score builds and bidding algorithms optimize on conversion data. Meta campaigns take 2–4 weeks to exit the learning phase — we plan for 60 days to see full channel performance.
