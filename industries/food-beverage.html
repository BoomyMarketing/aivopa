What We Cover

Food & Beverage Marketing Channels

Food is one of the most visual, sharable categories online — and one of the most competitive on Meta and TikTok. Winning requires authentic content that fits platform culture, precise audience targeting, and retention programs that turn first buyers into subscription revenue.

🍕

Meta & TikTok DTC Campaigns

Conversion campaigns optimized on first purchase and subscription starts. Recipe videos, taste-test reactions, and "clean ingredient" education content outperform product photography. UGC creative refreshed monthly. Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns for catalog brands with multiple SKUs.

🛒

Amazon Advertising

Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display campaigns for Amazon-listed products. Product listing optimization (title, bullets, A+ content, main images) before running ads. DSP retargeting for high-intent Amazon shoppers. Subscribe & Save conversion campaigns.

🍴

Food Influencer Campaigns

Food creators on TikTok and Instagram for recipe integration, taste tests, and product reviews. We source creators whose audience matches your target demographic — not just follower count. Content used as Spark Ads and Meta dark posts for 3–5x better performance than brand-shot creative.

🔄

Subscription & DTC Retention

Klaviyo flows for subscription welcome, churn prevention, win-back, and upsell. Subscription brands depend on LTV — we build retention programs that reduce churn by 20–30% and increase average subscription length. New flavor/SKU launch campaigns to existing subscriber lists.

🏪

Retail Velocity Campaigns

Geo-targeted Meta and Google campaigns near retail partner locations to drive in-store trial. "Find Us In Stores" landing pages with store locator. Shopper marketing campaigns coordinated with retailer promotional calendar. Off-shelf awareness that translates to retail shelf velocity.

📊

Attribution & LTV Modeling

DTC brands need to know 90-day and 12-month LTV by acquisition channel to set profitable CAC targets. We build LTV models from your order history data and use them to set channel-specific CAC targets — so you know exactly how much you can afford to spend to acquire a customer.

Our Process

How We Scale F&B Brands

A 60-day sprint to DTC ROAS target — content testing in the first 30 days, scaling in days 30–60, subscription and retention compounding from month one.

1

Brand & Category Audit

Map competitive brands' ad creative on Meta Ad Library and TikTok Creative Center. Identify the hero claim (taste, ingredient quality, convenience, health benefit, mission) that differentiates your product. Category is crowded — you need a clear point of view before running a single ad.

2

LTV & CAC Modeling

Pull your order history to calculate 30, 60, 90, and 180-day LTV by first-order product and acquisition cohort. This determines your profitable CAC ceiling — the maximum you can spend to acquire a customer and still be profitable. Everything else follows from this number.

3

UGC & Influencer Content Sprint

Source 8–12 food creators for authentic content: taste tests, recipes, unboxing, "reasons I switched" testimonials. 72-hour production turnaround. Test 5–6 hook variations in the first 2 weeks. Kill underperformers by CTR at day 7; scale winners by CPA at day 14.

4

DTC Campaign Launch

Meta Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns with UGC creative. Google Shopping for branded and category queries. TikTok Spark Ads amplifying top organic content. Retargeting sequences for add-to-cart and checkout abandonment — food brands typically see 20–30% cart abandonment recovery.

5

Subscription & Retention Setup

Klaviyo welcome series (3-touch, week 1). Subscription offer email at day 10 (after first order delivered). Churn prevention sequence for subscribers who skip 2 consecutive boxes. Win-back for lapsed subscribers with "What changed?" survey + offer.

6

Amazon & Retail Expansion

Amazon listing optimization and Sponsored Products launch. Coordinate retail velocity campaigns with retailer promotional calendar. Build "Find Us In Stores" Google and Meta campaigns geo-targeted within 5 miles of key retail locations. Track retail sell-through alongside DTC metrics.

FAQ

Food & Beverage Marketing Questions

What food and beverage categories do you work with?

Packaged food and snacks, beverages (functional drinks, RTD coffee, alcohol, kombucha, juice), supplements and nutrition products, meal kits and meal delivery, specialty and artisan food brands, plant-based and alternative protein, and condiments and pantry staples. Both DTC and retail-focused brands.

How do you drive DTC sales for food brands?

Meta and TikTok are the primary DTC channels for food brands — food content is inherently visual and viral. We run conversion campaigns with product-in-use video (recipe content, taste tests, unboxings) optimized on first purchase and subscription starts. Email/SMS retention is critical for food DTC: subscription boxes and replenishment programs drive 40–60% of revenue for established brands.

How does Amazon advertising work for CPG brands?

Amazon Sponsored Products and Sponsored Brands capture in-market buyers searching within Amazon's ecosystem. We optimize product listings (title, bullet points, A+ content, images) for conversion before running ads — listing quality is 70% of Amazon performance. We also run DSP campaigns retargeting Amazon shoppers off-platform on third-party sites.

How do you use influencers for food and beverage brands?

Food influencers on TikTok and Instagram drive product discovery through recipe videos, taste tests, and 'What I eat in a day' content. We source creators with genuine food audiences (not just lifestyle), brief them on hero claims and key differentiators, and use their content as Spark Ads and dark post creative. Food UGC generates 3–5x higher engagement than branded studio content.

How do you balance DTC and retail marketing for food brands?

DTC and retail are complementary, not competing channels. DTC builds brand awareness and customer data; retail scales volume. We run 'Find Us In Stores' campaigns for brands entering retail, and use DTC customer data to build retail shopper audiences for in-store promotion. Attribution between DTC and retail lift is tracked using geo-based incrementality testing.
