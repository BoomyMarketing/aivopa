FAQ

Landscaping Marketing Questions

Google Search captures homeowners and property managers actively searching for services: 'landscaping company near me,' 'lawn care service Long Island,' 'commercial landscaping NYC,' 'landscape design Westchester.' These are high-intent leads with short decision windows. Local Services Ads (Google Guaranteed) appear above all other search results for local service queries and only charge per lead — highly cost-efficient for landscapers. Local SEO drives organic rankings for neighborhood-specific queries. For commercial landscaping, LinkedIn and direct outreach to property managers and HOA decision-makers are critical for large contract acquisition.

Landscaping has two major marketing seasons in the NYC area. Spring (March–May) is the highest-volume period — homeowners call for clean-ups, lawn care programs, and landscape installations as the weather warms. We ramp up Google Search and LSA budgets 3–4 weeks before spring thaw to capture the first-movers who book before competitor schedules fill. Fall (September–October) is the second peak for clean-ups, aeration, and overseeding. Winter is used for snow removal leads (plowing, salting) and early spring pre-booking campaigns targeting homeowners planning landscape projects before the spring rush.

Commercial landscaping requires a different approach than residential. Property management companies, HOAs, commercial real estate owners, and corporate campuses are the key decision-makers. We target property managers on LinkedIn by company type and building portfolio size. Google Search captures property managers researching 'commercial lawn care NYC' or 'commercial landscaping contract New York.' Direct outreach with portfolio photos, insurance documentation, and references from comparable properties is critical for converting commercial prospects — they require more trust signals than residential clients. A single commercial account ($15,000–100,000/year) can anchor a landscaping business.

Reviews are critical for landscaping — homeowners making multi-hundred to multi-thousand dollar decisions almost always check Google and Yelp before calling. A landscaping company with 80+ Google reviews at 4.7+ stars gets significantly more calls from Google Business Profile than a company with 15 reviews at the same rating. We build systematic review generation into every campaign: post-job text/email sequences asking satisfied clients for a Google review, with direct link to the review form. Seasonal timing matters — ask for reviews immediately after a completed spring clean-up or installation while satisfaction is highest.

Google Search for landscaping keywords in the NYC area costs $4–18 per click, with CPL ranging from $30–90 for optimized campaigns. LSA (Google Guaranteed) charges $15–50 per qualified lead for landscaping — often the most cost-efficient paid channel for residential service calls. Most landscaping companies start with $1,000–3,000/month in ad spend. A recurring residential lawn care client ($1,500–5,000/year) or commercial account ($15,000–100,000/year) provides strong LTV relative to acquisition cost. We track calls, form leads, and revenue by channel — and report on cost per recurring account vs. cost per one-time job.
