FAQ

Electrician Marketing Questions

Google Search and Local Services Ads (LSA) are the top channels for electricians. Homeowners and property managers searching 'electrician near me,' 'licensed electrician NYC,' 'electrical panel upgrade Brooklyn,' or 'electrical outlet not working Manhattan' are in immediate need — these searches convert to calls within minutes. LSA (Google Guaranteed) appears above all other search results and charges per lead rather than per click, making it highly cost-efficient for electricians. Local SEO and Google Business Profile generate organic calls with no ongoing cost-per-click. Commercial electrical work requires separate LinkedIn and direct outreach strategies targeting construction managers, property managers, and GCs.

The NYC electrical market is competitive but fragmented — most small electrical contractors win on speed of response, local presence, and licensing credentials rather than marketing sophistication. Electricians who appear in Google's local 3-pack with strong reviews and fast response times consistently out-perform larger competitors who are slower to follow up. We build campaigns that prioritize call volume and response time infrastructure alongside the ads themselves — a lead that doesn't get called within 5 minutes converts at a fraction of the rate of one called immediately. Specialty positioning (EV charger installation, panel upgrades, smart home wiring) creates keyword categories with lower competition and higher ticket values.

Commercial electrical work — new construction, tenant improvements, building-wide upgrades — requires a different strategy than residential service calls. General contractors, property managers, commercial real estate owners, and building managers are the key decision-makers. Google Search for 'commercial electrician NYC' and 'electrical contractor New York' captures companies actively seeking bids. LinkedIn campaigns targeting GCs and property managers by company size build awareness. Direct outreach with completed project portfolios, insurance certificates, and bonding information converts commercial prospects who vet contractors thoroughly before awarding contracts.

NYC requires Master Electrician licensure for permitted electrical work — and homeowners and property managers increasingly know to verify this before hiring. We build campaigns around your licensing credentials, insurance, and bonding prominently: 'Licensed Master Electrician NYC,' 'Fully Insured & Bonded.' The Google Guaranteed badge on LSA provides additional trust verification. For commercial work, licensing documentation is often required before a contractor can bid — we create landing pages and materials that make verification easy for commercial prospects.

Google Search for electrician keywords in NYC costs $8–25 per click (competitive market), with CPL ranging from $40–120. LSA charges $20–60 per qualified lead — often more efficient than traditional Google Ads for residential service calls. Most electricians start with $1,000–3,000/month in ad spend and see 15–40 qualified leads per month. Residential jobs average $500–5,000 (service calls to panel upgrades), commercial projects $5,000–100,000+. Even at $80 CPL, a single panel upgrade ($2,000–4,000) delivers strong ROI. We track calls, form submissions, and revenue by channel.
