Our Process

How We Fill Your Caseload in 30 Days

1
Practice Audit & Ideal Client Profile

Review your current referral sources, specialty areas, insurance panels, and caseload capacity. Define your ideal client profile — presenting concern, demographics, insurance or self-pay preference — to shape targeting and messaging.

2
Keyword & Directory Research

Map search volume for your specialty-specific keywords in your service area. Audit your Psychology Today profile and competitive directory presence. Identify quick-win optimizations alongside paid campaign opportunities.

Landing Page & Tracking Setup

Build or optimize a specialty-specific landing page with HIPAA-compliant contact form, call tracking, and conversion events. Pages convert at 2–3x homepage rates because they speak directly to the client's presenting concern.

4
Campaign Launch

Launch Google Search with specialty-grouped campaigns. Optimize Psychology Today and key directory profiles simultaneously. First inquiry calls and contact forms arrive within days of launch.

6
Reporting & Caseload Optimization

Monthly reporting on inquiries, consultations booked, and new clients started by campaign. Adjust keyword bids and specialty focus based on which campaigns attract clients who convert to ongoing therapy — not just one-session contacts.

Mental Health Marketing Questions

Google Search is the highest-intent channel — someone searching 'therapist for anxiety NYC' or 'EMDR therapist Brooklyn' is ready to book. Local SEO and Google Maps ranking matter for neighborhood-level searches. Psychology Today directory optimization is table stakes for private practices. For group practices and telehealth providers, Google Search plus insurance-specific targeting can scale new client acquisition efficiently.

We use HIPAA-compliant tracking configurations that avoid passing PHI (protected health information) to ad platforms. We do not retarget based on health-related browsing behavior or use sensitive condition targeting in Meta campaigns. Call tracking is configured with HIPAA-compliant providers. We use aggregate conversion events (form submission, call, appointment) rather than individual-level health data. Compliance is built into our setup from day one, not added as an afterthought.

Both serve different timelines. Google Ads generates new client inquiries within days of launch — ideal for filling a new practice or addressing cancellations. SEO builds organic ranking over 3–6 months for sustainable long-term traffic. For most private practice therapists in NYC, we recommend starting with Google Ads to get immediate clients, while simultaneously building Local SEO so organic traffic reduces ad dependency over time. The combination creates both short-term and long-term lead flow.

Specialty targeting is core to our approach. We build separate Google Search campaigns for each specialty: anxiety therapy, depression treatment, couples counseling, trauma/EMDR, ADHD, eating disorders, etc. Each specialty has its own keywords, ad copy, and landing page that speaks directly to that client's situation. Specialty-specific campaigns convert at 2–3x the rate of generic 'therapist NYC' campaigns because the messaging resonates with exactly what the person is searching for.

Google Search for therapists in NYC typically costs $8–25 per click, with CPL (cost per new client inquiry) ranging from $40–120 depending on specialty and competition. At a $500/month ad spend, most solo therapists see 4–12 qualified inquiries per month. Group practices with higher budgets can scale predictably — 20–50+ new client inquiries monthly. Management fees vary by scope. A full therapy practice caseload can typically be built within 90–120 days of consistent marketing.
