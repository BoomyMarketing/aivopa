FAQ

Event Planning Marketing Questions

For corporate event planners: LinkedIn to reach executive assistants, marketing directors, and HR managers who book company events, and Google Search for high-intent queries like 'corporate event planner NYC' or 'company holiday party planning New York.' For social event planners (birthdays, mitzvahs, galas): Google Search, Pinterest for inspiration-stage discovery, and Meta targeting adults planning milestone events. For event venues: Google Search and Local SEO are primary, with Pinterest for visual discovery and social proof.

Event planning has multiple seasonal peaks. Corporate holiday party season (September–November, for December events) is the highest-volume corporate inquiry period. Social milestones (spring and fall bar/bat mitzvahs, summer galas, fall galas) have distinct inquiry timelines. We build campaigns that front-load budget 2–4 months before each peak event period — when clients are actively researching and booking. Off-peak months are used for SEO content, portfolio building, and LinkedIn brand awareness.

Corporate event marketing requires reaching the people who actually book company events: executive assistants, marketing and comms managers, HR directors, and C-suite at companies with meaningful event budgets. LinkedIn is the dominant channel for this audience — we run sponsored content and direct outreach targeting by job title, company size, and industry. Google Search captures companies actively researching planners. Content marketing (case studies, venue guides, event trend reports) builds authority and generates inbound from corporate event buyers.

Boutique and mid-size event planners compete by specializing: a corporate event company known for tech company offsites, or a social planner specializing in luxury bat mitzvahs, can dominate niche searches that large generalists ignore. We build campaigns around your specific event specialties and past portfolio — targeting the exact keywords and audiences where you have the best shot at winning. Niche authority beats generic competition in both search rankings and client conversion.

Google Search for event planning keywords in NYC typically costs $5–20 per click, with CPL (cost per inquiry) ranging from $50–200 depending on specialization and competition. LinkedIn for corporate event targeting costs more per click ($8–25) but delivers higher-budget inquiries. Most event planners see 5–20 qualified inquiries per month at moderate ad budgets ($1,000–3,000/month). A single corporate event contract ($20,000–100,000+) covers months of marketing investment. We track inquiries, proposals, and booked events by channel.
