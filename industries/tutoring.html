2

4

Monthly reporting on enrollments, program completion, and revenue by channel. Quarterly review of seasonal campaign performance vs. enrollment targets. Identify which exam seasons and programs produce the highest-LTV students — and allocate budget to maximize those cycles year over year.

FAQ

Tutoring Marketing Questions

Google Search is the primary channel — NYC parents actively search 'SAT tutor NYC,' 'SHSAT prep Brooklyn,' 'math tutor near me Manhattan,' and grade-specific queries year-round. These searches have clear purchase intent and short decision windows, especially during test registration periods. Meta campaigns are effective for reaching parents by neighborhood, school district, and child age — Facebook parent groups and Instagram ads build awareness before a test-prep need becomes urgent. Local SEO and Google Business Profile drive organic enrollments for neighborhood-specific queries.

Tutoring has distinct peaks tied to the NYC school calendar. SHSAT prep (for specialized high schools) is the highest-demand period — September through October when 8th graders register and study for the November exam. SAT/ACT prep peaks January–April (spring test dates) and August–October (fall test dates). Back-to-school (August–September) sees a broad spike in general subject tutoring. Regents prep surges in May–June. We build a 12-month campaign calendar that pre-loads budget 4–6 weeks before each peak, capturing parents when the decision urgency is highest.

Local and boutique tutoring centers win on specialization, relationships, and NYC-specific knowledge. A tutor who knows the SHSAT cold, or a center that has placed students in Stuyvesant and Bronx Science for 10 years, has a competitive advantage that Kumon and Sylvan can't replicate. We build campaigns around these local differentiators: NYC exam expertise, neighborhood presence, tutor credentials, and testimonials from local families. Niche specialization (SHSAT only, or AP courses only, or 3rd–5th grade math) dominates specific keyword clusters and commands higher prices.

Meta targeting by neighborhood, child age range, and parental income level reaches the parents most likely to invest in tutoring. We build custom audiences from school district ZIP codes and layer in behavioral signals (parent status, educational interest). Google Search captures parents already searching — we match ad copy to the specific exam or subject they're researching. Retargeting parents who visited your site but didn't enroll is especially effective for high-consideration tutoring decisions where parents compare 3–5 options before committing.

Google Search for tutoring keywords in NYC costs $3–12 per click, with CPL ranging from $40–120 depending on subject and exam type (SHSAT/SAT keywords are more competitive). Meta parent campaigns run $25–80 CPL. Most tutoring centers budget $1,000–3,000/month in ad spend and see 10–30 enrollment inquiries per month. A single student enrollment ($1,500–6,000 for a full prep course) covers months of marketing spend. We track inquiries, consultations, enrollments, and revenue by channel.
