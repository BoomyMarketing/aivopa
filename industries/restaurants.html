Restaurant Marketing Channels

NYC has over 25,000 restaurants competing for the same diners. The ones that win own local search, have a compelling social presence, and show up in the right place at the right moment — when someone is hungry and deciding.

Google Maps & Local SEO

Google Business Profile optimization: photos, menu, hours, Q&A, and review response strategy. Map Pack presence for "[cuisine] near me" searches drives 3x more reservations than organic website rankings alone.

Google Ads — Local Search

Search campaigns targeting high-intent queries: "[cuisine] NYC", "restaurants [neighborhood]", "restaurants near me", and competitor brand names. Call extensions and reservation links convert search intent directly into bookings.

Meta & Instagram Ads

Neighborhood-targeted Instagram ads showcasing food photography, ambiance, and events. Retargeting audiences of profile visitors and website browsers. Event promotion campaigns for private dining and catering inquiries.

Our Process

How We Fill Tables in NYC

Restaurant marketing in New York requires hyperlocal targeting, strong visual creative, and search intent capture — all working together across a 30-day activation sprint.

1

Local Search Audit

Audit your Google Business Profile completeness, review score vs. competitors, and current Map Pack rankings for your cuisine category and neighborhood. This is the highest-ROI starting point for most restaurants.

2

GBP Optimization

Update all GBP fields: menu, hours, photos (minimum 20 high-quality images), attributes, and service options. Set up Google Q&A, respond to all existing reviews, and establish a weekly review response cadence.

3

Google Ads Launch

Search campaigns for brand terms, cuisine category terms, and neighborhood intent. Call-only campaigns for mobile users. Conversion tracking via call extensions, reservation clicks, and form submissions.

4

Social Creative & Targeting

Build Meta/Instagram campaigns with neighborhood radius targeting (1–3 miles for delivery, 5 miles for dine-in). A/B test food photography vs. ambiance vs. chef-story creative to find the highest-engagement format for your brand.

5

Delivery Platform Setup

Activate DoorDash Ads and Uber Eats promotions. Optimize menu titles and descriptions for platform search keywords. Track delivery revenue net of commissions to ensure positive unit economics before scaling spend.

6

Reporting & Monthly Review

Weekly dashboard: reservations, calls, direction requests, delivery orders, and ad spend by channel. Monthly review to shift budget toward highest-performing channels and refresh creative as seasons and menus change.

FAQ

Restaurant Marketing Questions

What restaurant types do you work with?

Independent restaurants, multi-location groups, fast casual chains, fine dining, ghost kitchens, catering companies, and food & beverage brands with DTC or D2C components. We adapt strategy to your revenue model — dine-in covers, delivery orders, catering contracts, or all three.

How do you drive more reservations and walk-ins?

Google Ads targeting high-intent local searches ('restaurants near me', '[cuisine] NYC', 'restaurants [neighborhood]'), combined with Google Maps optimization (GBP photos, reviews, Q&A). Guests searching in-market convert at 3–5x the rate of social media audiences — search intent is the highest-value channel for restaurants.

Can you help with delivery platforms like DoorDash or Uber Eats?

Yes. We manage DoorDash Ads, Uber Eats promoted listings, and menu optimization to increase your platform visibility and order volume. We also track delivery revenue vs. dine-in to help you understand the true profitability of each channel — delivery commissions (25–30%) change the unit economics significantly.

How important is Instagram for restaurant marketing?

Instagram and TikTok drive discovery and brand desire, especially for NYC audiences. We run Meta/Instagram campaigns targeting local users by neighborhood, interest, and dining behavior. Content strategy (food photography, behind-the-scenes, UGC from guests) is briefed alongside paid — organic and paid work together to build the brand.

How do you measure restaurant marketing results?

Dine-in: reservation volume (OpenTable/Resy), call tracking, and Google Maps direction requests. Delivery: platform order volume and revenue. Catering: form submissions and inbound calls. We build a unified dashboard so you see exactly how each channel contributes to total revenue — not just impressions.
