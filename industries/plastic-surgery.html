FAQ

Plastic Surgery Marketing Questions

Google Search is the highest-intent channel — patients searching 'rhinoplasty NYC,' 'breast augmentation Manhattan,' 'mommy makeover New York,' or 'best plastic surgeon NYC' are actively researching procedures and comparing surgeons. These searches indicate a decision process that may take weeks or months but starts with high intent. Meta (Instagram especially) is critical for visual discovery — before/after galleries, procedure explainer content, and surgeon personality content build trust over the long consideration window. YouTube procedure explainer videos rank organically and generate consultation requests from well-researched patients. Retargeting is especially effective given the 2–6 month research cycle.

Plastic surgery decisions typically take 2–6 months from initial research to consultation booking — longer for major procedures (rhinoplasty, body contouring) and shorter for non-surgical treatments (Botox, fillers). Marketing must be present throughout this research window, not just at the moment of search. We build multi-touch campaigns: Google Search captures early research, Instagram and YouTube build surgeon trust over weeks, retargeting re-engages visitors who researched but didn't book, and email sequences nurture leads through to consultation. The surgeon who is most present and trustworthy across the research journey — not just the one running the most aggressive ads — wins the consultation.

Before/after content is the most powerful conversion driver for plastic surgery — patients want to see real results from real patients. Meta (Facebook and Instagram) prohibits before/after images in ad creative but allows them on your page and website. We build ad creative that drives traffic to gallery pages rather than showing before/after in the ads themselves. Google Display and YouTube allow before/after in specific formats. Your organic Instagram and website gallery are the most important assets — we optimize these to be easily discoverable and structured for maximum consultation conversion. Patient testimonial videos perform exceptionally well across all platforms.

Trust is the dominant conversion factor in plastic surgery — patients are making high-stakes, irreversible decisions and they research thoroughly. We build trust across multiple signals: board certification prominently featured (ABPS board certification is the gold standard), before/after galleries with diverse patient representation, video content showing surgeon personality and consultation approach, patient testimonials (written and video), press and media features, hospital affiliations, and detailed procedure pages that answer every research question before a patient has to ask. Surgeons who publish educational content — blog posts, YouTube procedure guides, Instagram Q&As — build organic authority that converts better than ads alone.

Google Search for plastic surgery keywords in NYC costs $10–40 per click (highly competitive), with CPL ranging from $80–250 for consultation requests. Meta campaigns for cosmetic surgery run $50–150 CPL for consultation inquiries. Most plastic surgery practices budget $3,000–8,000/month in ad spend. Given that procedure revenue ranges from $5,000 for rhinoplasty touch-ups to $30,000+ for comprehensive body contouring, even a $200 CPL at a 30% consultation-to-surgery conversion rate produces strong ROI. We track consultations booked, procedures performed, and revenue by marketing channel.
