FAQ

Catering Marketing Questions

It depends on whether you focus on corporate or social event catering. Corporate catering: LinkedIn is the top channel for reaching office managers, executive assistants, and HR managers who book company meals and events. Google Search captures companies searching 'corporate catering NYC' or 'office lunch delivery Manhattan.' Social event catering (weddings, galas, mitzvahs): Google Search for 'wedding catering NYC' or 'event catering New York,' Pinterest for visual inspiration-stage discovery, and The Knot/Zola for wedding marketplace visibility. Both tracks benefit from strong Google Business Profile and review presence. Seasonal campaigns (September–November for corporate holiday party season) are critical for both tracks.

Corporate catering accounts are the most valuable recurring revenue source — a company ordering weekly office lunches or quarterly events can be worth $25,000–200,000+ annually. LinkedIn is the dominant acquisition channel: sponsored content targeting office managers and executive assistants by company size and industry, and direct outreach to decision-makers at target accounts. Google Search captures companies actively seeking caterers for specific events. Once a corporate account is acquired, referrals within the company and to peer companies at the same stage are the most efficient growth mechanism — one happy corporate client regularly refers 3–5 peer companies.

Catering has two major peaks. Corporate holiday party season (September–November, for December events) is the highest-demand period for corporate catering — companies book caterers 6–8 weeks before their events. Spring (April–June) is the second peak for social events: weddings, graduation parties, spring galas. We build seasonal campaign calendars that ramp up 8–10 weeks before each peak, capturing early-planning companies and couples before competitor calendars fill. Year-round corporate lunch and recurring office catering programs smooth out the revenue between peaks.

Food photography is the single most important creative asset for catering marketing — clients buy with their eyes first. High-quality images of plated food, buffet setups, and live event service dramatically outperform text-heavy ads in both click-through rate and consultation conversion. We build catering marketing around your best food and event photography: Instagram content showcasing menus, event setups, and behind-the-scenes preparation; Pinterest boards organized by cuisine style and event type; and Google Business Profile photos updated seasonally. For catering companies without professional photography, we recommend investing in a photo shoot before launching paid campaigns.

Google Search for catering keywords in NYC costs $3–12 per click, with CPL ranging from $40–150 for catering inquiry requests. LinkedIn for corporate catering is more expensive per click ($8–20) but delivers higher-value corporate account inquiries. Most catering companies budget $1,500–4,000/month in ad spend. A single corporate holiday party contract ($8,000–50,000) or wedding catering contract ($15,000–80,000) provides strong ROI against even a $150 CPL. We track inquiries, proposals, and booked contracts by channel with revenue attribution reporting.
