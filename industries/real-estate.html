Who We Work With in Real Estate

Each segment requires a different channel mix, creative strategy, and lead qualification approach.

Buyer & Seller Lead Generation

Google Ads on property search queries and 'sell my home' intent captures buyers and sellers in active need. Meta Lead Gen campaigns with neighborhood market reports and home valuation offers generate seller leads efficiently. For buyer leads, we target by price range, bedrooms, and neighborhood preference signals. Lead forms are optimized for qualification — capturing timeline, pre-approval status, and price range to filter out casual browsers before leads hit your CRM. New Development Marketing

New development and pre-construction marketing builds a prospect database before keys exist — converting interest into reservations and sales center visits. We run Meta Lead Gen campaigns targeting buyers at the development's price point and demographic profile, Google Ads on neighborhood and 'new construction' queries, programmatic display on real estate research sites (StreetEasy, Zillow, Realtor.com), and YouTube pre-roll video walk-throughs of renderings. Prospect database campaigns are sequenced: awareness phase (building the list), nurture phase (floor plan updates, event invitations), and conversion phase (sales center appointments).

Meta + Google + programmatic + prospect database

Luxury Real Estate Marketing

We access luxury buyer audiences through: programmatic display on premium financial and lifestyle publications (WSJ.com, Bloomberg, Architectural Digest), LinkedIn targeting of high-income professional profiles (finance, law, tech executives in NYC metro), CTV advertising on premium streaming services, and Instagram visual campaigns for aspirational property showcase. Luxury campaigns emphasize lifestyle and investment narrative alongside property specifications.

Listing Retargeting

Listing retargeting serves ads for specific properties to users who previously viewed them on your website or MLS portal — the highest-intent audience in real estate. We build listing-specific retargeting audiences and serve dynamic creative featuring the viewed property (similar to ecommerce dynamic product ads, but for real estate). Listing retargeting consistently delivers the lowest cost-per-lead in real estate because it's reaching buyers who are actively evaluating a specific property — not cold traffic requiring awareness and consideration before expressing interest.

Our Process

Real Estate Marketing Methodology

Real estate marketing success is defined by lead quality, not lead volume. 100 low-intent leads that never respond to follow-up are worthless. 20 pre-approved buyers with a 90-day purchase timeline are a pipeline. We optimize toward the latter.

01

Lead Economics Modeling

Before setting campaign targets, we model your lead economics: average commission per transaction (GCI), close rate from marketing leads (typically 1–3% for cold paid media leads), and lead-to-appointment conversion rate. From these inputs we derive the maximum sustainable cost-per-lead — the amount you can spend on a lead and still generate positive ROI at your current close rate. This model prevents the common mistake of optimizing purely for low CPL when lead quality and close rate determine actual ROI. A $20 CPL that closes at 0.5% generates less revenue than a $50 CPL that closes at 3%.

02

Audience & Creative Strategy

Real estate creative must match the emotional moment of the buyer's journey. Early-stage buyers respond to neighborhood lifestyle content — schools, restaurants, walkability, community. Mid-stage buyers respond to specific property features and price positioning. Late-stage buyers respond to urgency signals — days on market, price reductions, comparison to recently sold comps. Seller campaigns use market data as creative — sold price vs. list price in the neighborhood, average days on market, current inventory levels — to demonstrate the agent's market knowledge and the favorable selling conditions. We develop creative strategies for each audience stage and test variants systematically.

03

Lead Capture & Qualification

Lead form design is the single biggest determinant of lead quality in real estate. Short forms (name + email only) generate high volume but low quality — lots of tire-kickers with no purchase timeline. Long forms (name, email, phone, price range, timeline, pre-approval status, current situation) generate lower volume but dramatically higher quality. We optimize form length based on your sales team capacity — if you can handle 200 leads/month with proper follow-up, shorter forms are fine; if you can only work 30 leads per month effectively, qualification questions are essential. CRM integration ensures leads are routed immediately with full context for follow-up within 5 minutes of submission.

04

Meta Fair Housing Compliance

Meta's Special Ad Category restrictions for housing require adjustments to standard campaign setup. We configure all real estate campaigns under the Housing special ad category: broad geographic targeting without neighborhood exclusions, no age or gender targeting, limited interest targeting options. Within these constraints, we use placement targeting (Instagram vs. Facebook reach differential), creative segmentation (different ads for different buyer profiles without demographic targeting), and lookalike audiences from your actual client database (building from quality seed audiences rather than demographic proxies). All campaigns are documented for fair housing compliance — protecting your brokerage from discrimination liability.

05

Follow-Up Speed Optimization

Real estate lead close rates drop dramatically with response time — leads responded to within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert than leads responded to within 30 minutes. We configure lead routing for speed: instant SMS notification to the agent's mobile when a new lead submits, CRM auto-enrollment in a lead nurture sequence (immediate email response, 2-day follow-up email, 1-week market update email) for leads that don't answer the initial call, and lead source tagging in CRM for attribution tracking. For brokerages routing leads to multiple agents, we implement lead distribution logic based on price range, geography, and agent availability.

06

Monthly Lead Quality Reporting

Monthly real estate marketing reports track the full funnel: leads generated by channel (volume and CPL), lead-to-appointment conversion rate, appointments set vs. contracts signed, GCI from marketing-sourced transactions, and marketing ROI (GCI divided by total marketing spend). We also report on lead quality indicators — phone number validity rate, response rate to follow-up, and timeline distribution (how many leads are ready to transact in 0–30 days vs. 3–6 months). Lead quality trends are more valuable than lead volume trends — a decline in lead quality signals creative or targeting issues before it shows up in your close rate 90 days later.

FAQ

Real Estate Marketing Questions Answered

What digital marketing channels work best for real estate lead generation?
For buyer leads: Google Ads on active property search queries delivers the highest purchase-intent traffic. Meta Lead Gen Forms are efficient with compelling property imagery and neighborhood market data as the lead magnet. Retargeting of listing viewers converts at 3–5x cold traffic. For seller leads: Google Ads on 'sell my home' and 'home value estimate' queries, Meta 'What's my home worth?' campaigns. For luxury real estate ($2M+): programmatic display on premium financial publications, LinkedIn for NYC finance/law/tech executives, and CTV on premium streaming.
How do you generate seller leads specifically?
Effective seller lead strategies: (1) Home valuation tool campaigns — 'What's your home worth?' ads driving to an AVM-powered instant valuation tool that captures homeowner contact info. (2) Expired listing campaigns — targeting homeowners whose listings expired on Zillow via programmatic display. (3) Facebook neighborhood targeting — reaching homeowners with market report content. (4) YouTube pre-roll for brand building — video campaigns positioning agents as local market experts. Seller leads require longer nurture cycles than buyer leads — most sellers need 6–18 months from first contact to listing appointment.
What are the real estate advertising restrictions on Meta?
Meta imposes Special Ad Category restrictions on real estate due to fair housing laws. Geographic targeting is restricted to a minimum radius (no neighborhood exclusions), age and gender targeting are disabled, and detailed interest targeting is limited. We work within these restrictions using broader geographic targeting, creative segmentation instead of audience segmentation, and placement targeting. All campaigns are configured under the Housing special ad category — protecting your brokerage from fair housing liability.
How much does real estate lead generation cost per lead?
NYC metro benchmarks: Buyer leads (Google Ads, any price range): $15–40 per form submission, with 20–40% qualification rate. Seller leads (home valuation campaigns): $10–25 per submission, with 10–20% qualifying as near-term listing opportunities. Luxury buyer leads ($2M+): $50–150 per qualified inquiry. New development prospect leads: $30–80 per prospect database opt-in. Cost-per-lead is only meaningful relative to close rate and commission — a $50 lead closing at 2% into a $1M commission is extraordinarily profitable.
How do you market a new development or condo building?
New development marketing builds a prospect database before opening: Meta Lead Gen targeting buyers in the development's price range and buyer profile, Google Ads on neighborhood and 'new condos' queries, programmatic display on Zillow and StreetEasy, YouTube pre-roll for video walk-throughs of renderings, and LinkedIn for commercial or luxury developments targeting high-income professionals. Campaigns are sequenced: awareness (building the prospect database), engagement (floor plan updates, event invitations), and conversion (sales center appointments and reservations).
