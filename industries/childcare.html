Our Process

1
Program & Capacity Audit

Map your age groups, capacity, current enrollment vs openings, and enrollment calendar. Define your ideal family profile — neighborhood, working parents, income bracket, curriculum preference. This shapes targeting and messaging for every campaign.

2
Parent Audience Build

Build Google and Meta audience segments targeting parents of the right age children within your service radius. Add custom audience layers from your current parent email list for lookalike expansion on Meta.

3
Landing Page & Tour Booking Setup

Build or optimize a tour booking landing page with curriculum highlights, staff bios, safety credentials, and online scheduling. Ensure mobile optimization — most childcare searches happen on smartphones during commutes or lunchtime.

4
Campaign Launch & Tracking

Launch Google Search and Meta campaigns. Set up tour booking tracking, form tracking, and phone call tracking. Immediate follow-up automation for new tour requests — response time within 30 minutes during business hours significantly improves conversion.

5
Review Generation System

Implement post-enrollment and anniversary review request sequences. Train your director team on optimal timing and approach. Build Google and care.com review volume systematically to dominate local search results during peak enrollment season.

6
Enrollment Reporting & Season Planning

Monthly reporting on tour bookings, enrollment conversions, and waitlist additions by channel. Plan next enrollment season campaign calendar 90 days in advance. Identify highest-converting neighborhoods and age groups to focus next season's budget.

FAQ

Childcare Marketing Questions

Google Search captures parents actively searching for childcare: 'daycare near me NYC,' 'preschool Brooklyn,' 'infant daycare Manhattan.' These searches have high intent — parents searching are ready to tour. Meta is highly effective for childcare because parents (particularly mothers aged 25–40) are active users and Meta allows precise targeting by parental status and child age. Local SEO and Google Maps dominate neighborhood-level searches. Reviews and word-of-mouth are the most trusted sources for childcare decisions — both must be systematically cultivated.

NYC childcare enrollment has two major peaks: fall enrollment (January–April, when parents search for September start dates) and mid-year enrollment (June–August, for September spots and January starts). We build a 12-month campaign calendar that front-loads budget during peak search windows, runs waitlist campaigns year-round for in-demand centers, and maintains baseline awareness campaigns between peaks. Summer and fall are also strong windows for after-school program enrollment.

Meta allows direct targeting by parental status and child age — we target parents of infants, toddlers, or preschool-age children within a specific radius of your center. Google Search is already parent-intent filtered by query. For preschool and pre-K programs, we target parents whose children are 2–4 years old. For after-school programs, parents of school-age children (5–12) within your service neighborhood. Each age group has distinct program messaging and enrollment timelines.

Childcare is the highest-trust purchase a parent makes — they're entrusting their child's daily care to you. Reviews on Google, Yelp, and care.com are the primary decision factor after word-of-mouth referral. Centers with 50+ 5-star reviews convert touring families at dramatically higher rates than centers with fewer reviews. We build systematic post-enrollment review request sequences and coach your team on review generation best practices that comply with platform policies.

Waitlist marketing focuses on two goals: converting waitlisted families before they find alternatives, and building a long waitlist that gives you flexibility to fill spots quickly when they open. We run 'join our waitlist' campaigns during peak enrollment season, maintain your Google and directory presence to capture organic waitlist additions, and build email nurture sequences that keep waitlisted families engaged and excited about your program until spots open. A managed waitlist is a competitive asset — we help you build and leverage it.
