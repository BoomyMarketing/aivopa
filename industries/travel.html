Travel Marketing Channels

Travel is one of the most competitive advertising categories — dominated by OTA giants with unlimited budgets. Winning requires focusing on what OTAs can't offer: specialized expertise, personalized service, and the ability to inspire travelers with authentic, story-driven content.

Our Process

How We Grow Travel Bookings

Travel marketing is seasonal, aspirational, and long-consideration. We build full-funnel programs that capture travelers at every stage — from dreaming to booking — and convert at the moment of highest intent.

1

Destination & Experience Audit

Map your top destinations and experiences by booking revenue and margin. Identify 3–5 specialty offerings where you can claim genuine expertise that OTAs and aggregators can't replicate. These become your primary keyword and content pillars.

2

Booking Tracking Setup

Connect your booking engine to Google Ads and Meta via conversion API. Track booking revenue, not just form submissions. Travel bookings happen 1–6 months after first ad exposure — multi-touch attribution is essential to understand the true contribution of each channel.

3

Content & Creative Strategy

Brief destination video content for TikTok and YouTube (authentic, not polished). High-resolution destination photography for Meta and Google Display. Traveler testimonials and story-driven copy for landing pages. Visual quality is the primary trust signal in travel — invest here first.

4

Seasonal Campaign Calendar

Map booking windows to travel seasons: January–March for summer bookings, September–October for holiday travel, March–April for fall season. Build campaigns 8–10 weeks before peak booking windows. Early-bird campaigns with deposit pricing create urgency before the window closes.

5

OTA Competition Strategy

Brand protection campaigns to prevent OTAs from conquesting your brand terms. Direct booking incentives (exclusive pricing, complimentary additions) featured in ad copy. Messaging that emphasizes advisor value, personalization, and what OTAs can't offer for complex itineraries.

6

Past Traveler Reactivation

Email and Meta retargeting campaigns targeting past clients for repeat bookings. Past travelers convert at 4–6x the rate of cold audiences — and at dramatically lower CAC. Seasonal reactivation campaigns ("It's been a year since your Italy trip — ready to plan your next adventure?") drive consistent repeat bookings.

FAQ

Travel Marketing Questions

What travel businesses do you work with?

Independent travel agencies, luxury travel advisors, tour operators, boutique hotels and resorts, vacation rental companies, cruise lines, destination management companies (DMCs), and travel technology platforms. We adapt strategy to your booking model — direct bookings, OTA competition, lead generation for custom itineraries, or B2B corporate travel.

How do you compete with OTAs like Expedia and Booking.com on Google?

Head-to-head bidding against OTAs on generic destination terms is rarely cost-effective for smaller travel brands. We focus on differentiation keywords: specialty experiences ('private safari Kenya', 'bespoke Italy honeymoon'), brand queries (protecting your own brand from OTA conquest), and advisor-value queries ('luxury travel agent NYC', 'custom itinerary planner'). We help you win where the OTAs don't play.

How does TikTok work for travel marketing?

TikTok is the #1 travel inspiration platform for Millennial and Gen Z travelers — 'TikTok made me book it' is a real phenomenon. Destination videos, travel tips, hidden gem content, and packing hacks drive massive organic reach that feeds paid campaigns. We run TikTok Spark Ads amplifying your best-performing organic content, plus In-Feed ads for direct booking campaigns.

How do you track bookings and revenue from travel ad campaigns?

We implement booking engine conversion tracking (whether you use Rezdy, FareHarbor, Checkfront, or a custom booking system), call tracking for phone bookings, and form submission tracking for custom inquiry leads. Revenue attribution — connecting which campaign produced which booking and at what value — is the foundation of profitable travel marketing.

How do you handle the seasonal nature of travel demand?

Travel has pronounced booking windows — peak booking for summer travel happens January–March; holiday travel books September–October. We build 'Book Early' campaigns that run 3–4 months before peak travel periods, then shift to last-minute deal campaigns as departure dates approach. Off-season campaigns focus on shoulder-season travel and future bookings, keeping pipeline full year-round.
