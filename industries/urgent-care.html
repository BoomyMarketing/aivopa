Urgent Care Marketing Questions

Google Search and Google Business Profile are the two highest-ROI channels for urgent care — most patients searching "urgent care near me NYC" or "walk-in clinic open now Brooklyn" make a decision within seconds based on what appears at the top of the map pack. Search ads capture patients who are searching right now with high urgency. GBP with current hours, wait times (if available), insurance accepted, and strong reviews is the critical conversion layer that turns a search into a walk-in. We also run targeted campaigns for specific services like STI testing, physicals, occupational health, and COVID testing that have predictable seasonal or steady-state demand.
Urgent care wins on the middle ground between ER and telehealth — faster and less expensive than an ER, more capable and in-person than telehealth. We position this clearly in ad copy: "No ER wait times. No ER prices." For patients with real but non-life-threatening conditions — sprains, infections, lacerations, fever — urgent care is the right choice, and we make that clear in targeting. Against telehealth, we emphasize on-site testing, X-ray capability, and same-day results that virtual visits can't provide.
Extremely important. For urgent care, availability is often the primary conversion factor — a patient in pain or with a sick child will choose the closest clinic that has short wait times and is open now. We optimize GBP to display accurate hours including extended evening and weekend hours, use Google's 'Temporarily Closed' and 'More Hours' features correctly, and where possible integrate live wait-time data. Ad copy that features short wait times ("Usually seen in under 30 minutes") and extended hours ("Open until 9 PM daily") outperforms generic ads by 2–4×.
High-volume standalone services worth separate campaigns include: occupational health and workers' comp (consistent B2B volume from local employers), sports physicals and back-to-school physicals (seasonal spikes in August–September), STI and sexual health testing (steady-state demand, high search volume), COVID and flu testing (seasonal), and immigration physicals (steady niche with specific query intent). Each of these has distinct search behavior and conversion patterns — separating them into dedicated campaigns improves quality scores and reduces cost-per-visit versus routing everything to a generic urgent care landing page.
Google Search CPCs for urgent care terms in NYC range from $3–15. CPL typically runs $20–60 per new patient visit. Most urgent care clinics start with $2K–6K/month in ad spend across Google Search and GBP optimization. With average revenue per visit running $150–400 and high repeat visit rates for occupational health and direct primary care patients, marketing ROI is strong for well-attributed campaigns.
