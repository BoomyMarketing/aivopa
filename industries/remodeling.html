FAQ

Remodeling Contractor Marketing Questions

Google Search is the primary channel for remodeling lead generation — homeowners and co-op owners searching "kitchen renovation NYC" or "bathroom remodel contractor Manhattan" are in active research mode with high intent. Houzz is the specialty platform where remodeling contractors build portfolio credibility with in-market homeowners browsing renovation inspiration. Local SEO and Google Business Profile matter for neighborhood-level discovery, especially in dense residential areas like Park Slope, the Upper West Side, and Cobble Hill where word-of-mouth and proximity drive contractor selection. Meta ads work for retargeting website visitors who viewed specific room types (kitchen, bathroom) but didn't request a consultation.
Kitchen and bathroom renovations generate the highest search volume and the best margins in NYC residential remodeling. Full apartment gut renovations are the highest-ticket category — $100K–500K+ jobs typically sourced through referrals and architect relationships, but also through Google Search for "gut renovation contractor NYC." NYC-specific projects worth dedicated campaigns include co-op kitchen renovations (co-op board approval required — we target owners who already have approval), brownstone restoration, and pre-sale renovation (targeting homeowners listing in 3–6 months who want to maximize sale price).
Co-op and condo renovation in NYC has unique constraints — board approval, alteration agreements, permitted work windows — and owners who understand these requirements are more valuable leads. We target co-op owners specifically with ad copy that acknowledges the process ("board-approved renovation contractors NYC," "licensed and insured for co-op alterations") to pre-qualify intent and filter out leads who don't understand the requirements. Real estate agent relationships and architect referral partnerships also drive high-quality co-op renovation leads that don't require ad spend.
Remodeling in NYC has a 4–16 week sales cycle from first inquiry to signed contract — longer than most service businesses. Homeowners compare multiple contractors, wait for permits and board approvals, and consult architects or designers before committing. We build remarketing campaigns that keep your brand visible during this consideration window — showing project photos and client testimonials to prospects who visited your site weeks ago and are still comparing. Following up with unsold estimates via email at 2, 4, and 8 weeks significantly increases close rates on high-intent leads who went quiet.
Google Search CPCs for remodeling terms in NYC range from $8–35 per click. CPL typically runs $80–250 per qualified estimate request. Most remodeling contractors start with $2.5K–8K/month in ad spend. With average kitchen renovations running $40K–120K and full gut renovations $100K–500K+, even a $500 CPL produces strong ROI if one in three estimates converts. We track from ad click through submitted estimate through signed contract — so every optimization is based on project revenue, not just leads.
