Our Process

How We Grow Your Veterinary Practice in 30 Days

1
Practice Profile & Species Specialty Audit

Define your practice type (general, emergency, specialty, exotic animal), species focus, and service offerings. Identify your highest-margin services and the client profiles most likely to have long-term relationships. Emergency and specialty practices require different keyword strategies and landing pages than general wellness practices.

2
GBP Audit & Review Gap Analysis

Benchmark your Google Business Profile against the top 3 competitor practices in your service area. Identify review count gaps, photo quality gaps, and service listing completeness. GBP optimization is the fastest ROI activity for most vet practices — improvements show results within 30–60 days without any ad spend.

3
Google Search Campaign Launch

Launch service-segmented campaigns: wellness visits, dental, surgery, and emergency (24/7). Each campaign drives to dedicated landing pages with booking CTAs, team photos, species-specific service lists, and client testimonials. Call tracking numbers connect every phone inquiry to its campaign.

4
Review Generation System Setup

Implement automated post-visit text and email review request sequences — sent 24 hours after discharge. Direct review link to Google (primary) and Yelp (secondary). Staff training on in-person review requests at checkout. Target: 5+ new Google reviews per month minimum.

5
Meta Awareness & New Mover Campaigns

Launch Facebook and Instagram awareness campaigns targeting pet owners in your service ZIP codes. New mover targeting reaches recently relocated pet owners who need a new vet and haven't established a local relationship yet. Retargeting campaigns convert website visitors who researched your services but didn't book.

6
New Client & Revenue Attribution

Monthly reporting on new clients, appointment types, and first-visit revenue by channel. Track 90-day retention rate by acquisition source — identify which channels produce loyal clients vs. one-time visits. Shift budget toward channels producing the highest-LTV new client relationships.

Veterinary Marketing Questions

Google Search and Local SEO are the dominant channels — pet owners searching 'vet near me,' 'dog vet Brooklyn,' 'cat veterinarian Manhattan,' or 'emergency vet NYC' are making immediate or near-term decisions. Google Business Profile ranking in the local 3-pack is the single most valuable position for most NYC vet practices. Meta campaigns (Facebook and Instagram) are effective for building neighborhood awareness and reaching new pet owners who've recently moved to an area and need a new vet. Emergency and specialty vet practices benefit heavily from Google Search for urgent-intent queries.

New pet owner acquisition in NYC happens primarily through search (Google organic + Google Ads), neighborhood referrals, and word of mouth from existing clients. The highest-leverage marketing activities are: (1) Google Business Profile optimization and review generation — GBP ranking for 'vet near me' is the top driver of new client calls in most NYC neighborhoods; (2) Google Search Ads for new-to-area pet owners who don't yet have a trusted vet; (3) Meta campaigns targeting new movers and recent pet adopters. New client welcome packages and automated follow-up protocols retain new clients after the first visit.

Emergency vet marketing requires being visible exactly when a pet owner has an urgent situation — which means dominating Google Search for 'emergency vet NYC,' '24 hour animal hospital Brooklyn,' 'emergency animal hospital near me.' These searches happen at 2am and on weekends — campaigns must run 24/7 with call tracking enabled. Specialty vet practices (oncology, cardiology, orthopedics) require a referral network strategy alongside Google Search — DVM referral marketing and educational content for general practice vets are critical for specialty case volume.

Reviews are critical — pet owners treat their animals like family and research vets as carefully as they'd research a pediatrician. A vet practice with 200+ Google reviews at 4.8 stars generates significantly more new client calls than a comparable practice with 30 reviews. NYC is a high-review market where pet owners write reviews readily when asked. We build automated post-visit review request systems — a text or email sent 24 hours after discharge asking for a review — that generate 3–5x more reviews than practices that rely on organic asks alone.

Google Search for vet keywords in NYC costs $3–12 per click for general vet queries, $15–35 for emergency vet searches. CPL ranges from $30–80 for well-optimized campaigns. Most vet practices see $8,000–25,000+ in annual LTV per new client relationship (lifetime pet care, multi-pet households). A $60 cost per new client delivers 100–400x ROI over a long-term client relationship. We track new client appointments, appointment types, and revenue attribution by channel with monthly reporting.
