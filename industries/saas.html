SaaS Marketing Services

How We Grow SaaS Companies

SaaS marketing requires a different playbook than ecommerce or lead generation. Longer sales cycles, multiple decision-makers, ACV-dependent channel economics, and the need to prove ROI through a CRM — not just an ad platform — demand a specialized approach that understands how SaaS businesses actually work.

💻

SaaS Paid Search

Google and Microsoft Ads capture SaaS buyers actively researching your category — the highest-intent traffic available. We target three query tiers: category queries (buyers defining the problem — 'CRM software for startups'), competitor queries (buyers evaluating alternatives — 'Salesforce alternative for SMB'), and integration/trigger queries (buyers in specific buying contexts — 'Slack CRM integration'). Each tier requires dedicated landing pages, ACV-appropriate CTA (free trial vs. demo request vs. contact sales), and lead qualification logic that routes prospects appropriately.

Category + competitor + integration query targeting

LinkedIn for SaaS

LinkedIn is the only channel where you can target SaaS buyers by company size, job title, seniority, industry, and specific technology stack simultaneously. We run Sponsored Content for thought leadership and direct-response lead generation, Lead Gen Forms (pre-filled with LinkedIn profile data — converting at 3x standard landing pages), and Conversation Ads for personalized outreach to warm audiences. For mid-market and enterprise SaaS, we run LinkedIn ABM programs targeting specific company accounts with coordinated content across multiple decision-maker personas.

Sponsored Content + Lead Gen Forms + ABM targeting

SaaS Account-Based Marketing

For SaaS companies with ACV above $25K, ABM is the most efficient path to enterprise pipeline. We build ABM programs using LinkedIn company targeting (reaching all relevant personas at target accounts), programmatic IP targeting (display ads to visitors from target company IP ranges), and intent data platforms (Bombora, G2, TechTarget) to identify accounts actively researching your category. ABM content includes role-specific messaging (different value proposition for the CTO, CFO, and end-user persona at the same account) and coordinated sales outreach timing that strikes when intent signals are highest.

LinkedIn + IP targeting + Bombora intent data

Free Trial & PLG Marketing

Product-led growth companies need a different marketing model — instead of generating leads for sales, you're driving trial signups and improving trial-to-paid conversion. We run Google Ads on 'free trial' and 'try' intent queries, G2 review site optimization to appear in comparison searches, and retargeting campaigns specifically targeting trial users who haven't activated key features. Post-signup email sequences (handled via product marketing integration) guide new users to the 'aha moment' that predicts paid conversion. We also identify product-qualified leads (PQLs) from in-product behavior data and route them to sales for timely outreach.

Trial acquisition + activation + PQL identification

Our Process

SaaS Marketing Methodology

SaaS marketing that drives MRR growth requires ICP precision, channel economics matched to ACV, and attribution that connects marketing spend to actual closed revenue. We build programs around your specific unit economics — not generic SaaS benchmarks.

01

ICP & ACV-Based Channel Strategy

We start by defining your ideal customer profile (company size, industry, tech stack, growth stage, buyer personas) and mapping it to channel economics. ACV determines which channels make sense: sub-$5K ACV supports self-serve Google Ads and G2 investment; $5K–25K ACV supports LinkedIn Sponsored Content and content syndication; $25K+ ACV supports LinkedIn ABM, intent data platforms, and coordinated sales-marketing ABM programs. We build a channel architecture that matches your ACV economics — not one that would require $1M in marketing to generate $100K in MRR.

02

Keyword & Competitive Research

SaaS keyword strategy covers three layers: category intent (buyers defining the problem space — highest volume, lowest intent), solution intent (buyers researching solution types — medium volume, high conversion potential), and competitor intent (buyers in active vendor evaluation — lowest volume, highest conversion rate). We map your keyword universe across all three layers, identify which competitor terms are most winnable based on your specific value proposition, and build keyword-to-landing-page-to-offer architecture ensuring every query type routes to the most appropriate conversion path for that buyer stage.

03

Conversion Architecture

SaaS conversion architecture decisions (free trial vs. demo request vs. pricing page vs. contact sales) must match the buyer's stage and your sales motion. Bottom-funnel high-intent buyers (competitor queries, pricing queries) should convert to demo request or free trial — not a generic 'contact us'. Mid-funnel awareness buyers (category queries) convert better to a content offer (ROI calculator, benchmark report) that provides value before asking for a sales conversation. We audit and optimize your entire conversion path: landing page message match to ad, CTA type by buyer intent, form field optimization, and follow-up speed (SaaS leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to enter the sales cycle).

04

CRM & Attribution Setup

Before spending significantly on demand generation, we configure the attribution infrastructure: UTM parameter taxonomy (consistent source/medium/campaign/content tagging across all channels), CRM campaign influence setup (tracking every marketing touchpoint in Salesforce or HubSpot, not just first or last touch), lead source integrity (preventing manual overrides that corrupt attribution data), and pipeline stage definitions aligned between marketing and sales (what exactly constitutes an SQL vs. SQO in your sales process). Clean attribution from day one prevents the 'we're not sure what's driving pipeline' problem that afflicts most SaaS marketing organizations.

05

Campaign Launch & Optimization

SaaS campaigns launch with conservative budgets and aggressive learning objectives — identifying which ICP segments, message angles, and channels generate SQLs (not just MQLs) before scaling spend. We run weekly optimizations: pausing ad groups with high MQL volume but poor MQL-to-SQL conversion (volume isn't valuable if sales rejects the leads), shifting budget to ICP-matched audiences that generate accepted SQLs, and testing new competitor targeting as competitive landscape shifts. SaaS optimization is slower than ecommerce — qualification signals take 2–4 weeks to emerge from sales cycle data — but the payoff from optimizing to SQL quality is a dramatic improvement in blended cost-per-revenue-dollar.

06

Monthly Pipeline Review

Monthly SaaS marketing reviews connect every dollar of marketing spend to pipeline outcomes: cost-per-MQL by channel and campaign (with MQL-to-SQL rate breakdown), cost-per-SQL by channel, pipeline generated (total ACV of SQLs from marketing sources), pipeline won (closed-won MRR from marketing-sourced pipeline), and marketing-sourced MRR as % of total new MRR. We also review the CAC payback period trend — if your customer acquisition cost is rising faster than LTV, scaling spend is making the problem worse, not better. Monthly reviews catch this trajectory early and prescribe corrections before it becomes a board-level problem.

FAQ

SaaS Marketing Questions Answered

What marketing channels work best for SaaS companies?
Channel effectiveness depends on ACV. For SMB SaaS (under $10K ACV): Google Ads on solution-aware queries, G2 and Capterra review ads, and Meta Ads for product awareness. For mid-market ($10K–100K ACV): LinkedIn Sponsored Content, Google Ads on competitor and integration queries, and content syndication via TechTarget or Bombora intent data. For enterprise ($100K+ ACV): LinkedIn ABM coordinated with SDR outreach, executive-targeted retargeting, and thought leadership amplification. SaaS buyers research on G2 and Google before visiting your site — presence at those research moments matters most.
How do you measure SaaS marketing ROI?
SaaS marketing ROI requires tracking through the entire funnel from first touch to closed MRR. Metrics we report: Cost-per-MQL and MQL-to-SQL conversion rate by channel, Cost-per-SQL, Pipeline generated (ACV of SQLs by marketing source), Closed-won MRR from marketing-sourced pipeline, and CAC payback period. We configure Salesforce or HubSpot attribution to track every marketing touchpoint to closed-won — so you see which campaigns generate MRR, not just form fills.
What is product-led growth (PLG) marketing?
PLG is a go-to-market strategy where the product drives acquisition, activation, and expansion — users sign up for a free trial or freemium plan, experience value, and convert to paid without a sales call. PLG marketing drives free trial signups, improves trial-to-paid conversion, and identifies product-qualified leads (PQLs) for targeted sales outreach. Tactics include Google Ads on 'free' and 'try' intent queries, G2 review visibility for competitive trial comparison, and retargeting of trial users who haven't activated key features.
How do you approach SaaS competitor keyword targeting?
Competitor keyword targeting captures buyers who are solution-aware and actively evaluating alternatives — the highest-intent SaaS paid search traffic. We target: direct brand terms ('[Competitor] alternative', '[Competitor] pricing'), comparison queries ('[Competitor] vs [your brand]'), and migration queries ('[Competitor] migration', 'switch from [Competitor]'). Each query type requires different ad copy and landing pages — conversion rates are proportionally higher because buyer intent is stronger, justifying the premium CPCs.
What SaaS marketing budget do I need to see results?
Minimums for meaningful results: Google Ads for SaaS keywords ($5,000+/mo — competitive SaaS terms cost $15–80 CPC), LinkedIn Ads ($5,000+/mo for meaningful professional reach), G2 and Capterra ads ($2,000+/mo to maintain review site visibility), retargeting ($1,000+/mo). For a comprehensive demand gen program, budget $15,000–30,000/mo in ad spend. At lower budgets, focus exclusively on bottom-funnel capture (branded search, competitor terms, G2) before expanding to awareness-stage LinkedIn campaigns.
