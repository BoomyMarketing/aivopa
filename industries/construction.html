What We Cover

Construction Marketing Channels

Construction marketing splits sharply between residential and commercial. Residential buyers search Google and respond to visual portfolio content. Commercial clients — developers, asset managers, corporations — are found on LinkedIn and close through relationship-based sales. We build both.

🏗️

Google Search — Residential

High-intent residential queries: "general contractor NYC", "home renovation contractor Manhattan", "kitchen remodel contractor Brooklyn", "luxury home builder". Call and form extensions. Landing pages with project portfolio, licensing credentials, and client testimonials.

💼

LinkedIn — Commercial B2B

Sponsored Content and Message Ads targeting property developers, commercial real estate owners, facility managers, and corporate real estate teams. ABM campaigns for specific target accounts. Thought leadership content from principals builds the relationship before the RFP.

🔍

Construction SEO

Service pages optimized for "[project type] contractor NYC" queries. Local SEO for borough-level searches. Case study content showcasing completed projects with scope, timeline, and client outcomes. Google Business Profile optimization for local residential search visibility.

📸

Portfolio & Visual Content

Professional project photography and drone footage for Meta, LinkedIn, and website. Before/after renovation sequences for residential campaigns. Time-lapse construction videos for commercial project showcases. Portfolio quality is the primary trust signal — this investment pays for itself.

📞

Lead Tracking & CRM

CallRail tracking per campaign with call recording. CRM integration (Salesforce, HubSpot, or Buildertrend) to track leads through estimate, proposal, and contract signing. Monthly reporting on cost-per-signed-contract by channel — the only metric that matters in construction marketing.

🏆

Awards & Industry Positioning

Content strategy around industry awards (AGC, BOMA, Procore Impact Awards), safety certifications, and sustainability credentials. These third-party trust signals matter significantly in commercial construction procurement — we feature them prominently in all marketing materials.

Our Process

How We Grow Construction Businesses

Residential and commercial construction require different programs running simultaneously. We build both tracks from week one.

1

Segment & Project Type Audit

Identify your most profitable project types by size, margin, and win rate. Residential remodels, commercial TI, new construction, and specialty trades have completely different buyer journeys. We focus marketing budget on the project categories where your win rate is highest.

2

Portfolio Content Development

Brief project photography and video for your top 5–8 completed projects. Drone footage where possible. Written case studies with project scope, square footage, budget range (if shareable), timeline, and client outcome. This content fuels every channel — website, ads, LinkedIn, and email.

3

Google Search Launch

Residential search campaigns with project-type ad groups. Location targeting by borough with bid adjustments for Manhattan (highest project values). Call-only campaigns for mobile searches. Landing pages per project category — not the generic homepage.

4

LinkedIn Commercial Program

Build target account list of developers, property managers, and corporate real estate teams in NYC. Sponsored Content campaign with project portfolio showcases. InMail outreach from principal profile to warm the relationship before a business development call. 90-day nurture sequence.

5

CRM & Proposal Tracking

Connect ad leads to CRM. Track through estimate request, site visit, proposal, and contract. Report on cost-per-estimate and cost-per-signed-contract monthly. Construction has high lead-to-estimate rates but variable close rates — tracking the full funnel reveals where deals are being lost.

6

Referral & Relationship Program

Past client email sequences for referral requests. Architect and designer relationship campaigns (they're the gatekeepers for high-end residential). Subcontractor network outreach for commercial partnership development. Referrals close at 5–10x the rate of cold leads in construction.

FAQ

Construction Marketing Questions

What construction sectors do you work with?

General contractors (residential and commercial), specialty trades (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, concrete, steel), design-build firms, commercial tenant improvement contractors, luxury residential builders, modular construction companies, and construction technology platforms. Strategy differs significantly between B2C residential and B2B commercial — we build separate programs for each.

How do you generate commercial construction leads?

Commercial construction leads come from B2B channels: LinkedIn targeting property developers, asset managers, facility managers, and corporate real estate teams; Google Search for commercial GC and design-build queries; and industry-specific platforms (Dodge Construction Network, ConstructConnect). Decision cycles are long (6–18 months) — we build nurture sequences matched to the typical procurement timeline.

How do you generate residential construction leads?

Residential leads come primarily from Google Search ('general contractor NYC', 'home renovation contractor Manhattan', 'luxury home builder Long Island') and Google Maps. Houzz advertising is effective for high-end residential. Meta campaigns work for mid-market residential remodeling — especially before/after project photo campaigns that showcase portfolio quality.

How long does construction lead generation take to produce results?

Residential construction leads from Google Search can appear within 48–72 hours of launch. Commercial construction has a much longer cycle — LinkedIn and Google campaigns build pipeline over 60–120 days before RFP invitations or direct project inquiries materialize. We set expectations upfront based on your project type and typical sales cycle.

How do you showcase construction portfolio in ads?

Portfolio quality is the primary trust signal in construction marketing. We develop photo and video content from completed projects — drone footage, before/after sequences, and time-lapse construction videos — for use in Meta, YouTube, and LinkedIn campaigns. Project case studies with scope, timeline, and client testimonials are used as landing page content and retargeting creative.
