1

Keyword & Competitor Research

Research search volume for photography keywords in NYC by specialty and borough. Identify gaps where your specific style or specialty can rank without competing against high-authority generalists. 3
Google Search & Landing Page Launch

Launch specialty-segmented Google Search campaigns. Build landing pages for each major specialty showing relevant portfolio galleries, package options, process overview, and testimonials. 4
Pinterest & Directory Optimization

SEO Content & Blog Build

Publish 4–6 optimized real-event blog posts targeting venue-specific and style-specific long-tail searches. 6
Inquiry & Revenue Reporting

Monthly reporting on inquiries, consultations, and bookings by channel. Track average contract value by source — some channels deliver volume, others deliver premium clients. Reallocate budget toward highest-revenue channels and identify your fastest-growing inquiry sources each quarter.

FAQ

Photography Marketing Questions

It depends on photography specialty. Wedding photographers: Pinterest for early inspiration discovery, Google Search for high-intent queries ('wedding photographer NYC,' 'Brooklyn wedding photographer'), and The Knot/Zola for marketplace visibility. Portrait photographers: Google Search for local discovery, Instagram for portfolio visibility, and Meta retargeting for people who visited the website. Commercial photographers: LinkedIn for reaching creative directors and marketing managers, Google Search for brand shoot and headshot queries, and portfolio SEO targeting specific commercial keywords. All specialties benefit from a strong Google Business Profile with reviews.

Wedding photography in NYC is highly competitive, but also highly searched — couples planning NYC weddings research photographers 12–18 months in advance. The most effective combination: Google Search targeting 'wedding photographer NYC' and venue-specific queries ('Central Park wedding photographer,' 'Brooklyn wedding photographer'), Pinterest presence with galleries organized by venue and style, and active presence on The Knot and Zola where NYC couples actively search and contact photographers. SEO-optimized blog posts from actual NYC weddings ('Real Wedding at [Venue Name]') build organic ranking over time and showcase portfolio to couples in the research phase.

Commercial photography buyers — creative directors, marketing managers, art directors — respond to different signals than consumer clients. LinkedIn is the dominant channel for reaching agency and brand buyers: sponsored content with portfolio case studies, campaign work highlights, and thought leadership. Google Search captures companies actively searching for headshot photographers, product photographers, or editorial photographers in NYC. Direct outreach to creative agencies and brand marketing teams, combined with a portfolio optimized for commercial keywords, generates the highest-value commercial inquiries. Specialty positioning (e-commerce product photography, corporate headshots, food photography) creates keyword clusters where you can rank ahead of generalists.

NYC has thousands of photographers, but most compete on price rather than specialization. Photographers who define a clear specialty and aesthetic — 'documentary-style NYC wedding photographer,' 'natural light family portraits Brooklyn,' 'luxury hotel food photography Manhattan' — dominate niche searches that general photographers ignore. We build campaigns around your specific style, specialization, and past portfolio rather than generic 'photographer NYC' keywords. Niche positioning also attracts better-fit clients who value your specific work, leading to higher inquiry-to-booking conversion rates and higher average contract values.

Google Search for photography keywords in NYC costs $2–10 per click with CPL ranging from $30–100 for optimized campaigns. Pinterest ads for wedding photography run $0.50–2 per click and generate lower-funnel inquiries over a 6–12 month consideration window. The Knot featured listings in NYC run $1,000–4,000/year but provide marketplace visibility to actively searching couples. Most photographers see 5–20 qualified inquiries per month at moderate budgets ($500–2,000/month). A single wedding booking ($3,000–8,000+) or commercial shoot ($2,000–15,000+) covers months of marketing spend. We track inquiries, consultations, and bookings by channel.
