Our Process

How We Fill Wedding Calendars in 30 Days

1
Vendor Audit & Season Planning

Review your current booking calendar, peak availability, and past inquiry sources. Build a 12-month campaign calendar aligned with engagement season, spring booking, and your specific venue or service availability.

2
Keyword & Audience Research

Identify high-intent search terms for your specific category and New York market. Map search volume to seasonal peaks. Build Meta and Pinterest audiences from past client data.

3
Visual Creative & Portfolio Prep

Brief a photography/creative strategy optimized for ads: portfolio selects for Pinterest, Reels for Meta, hero shots for Google Display. Wedding marketing lives and dies on visual quality.

4
Campaign Launch & Tracking

Launch Google Search, Meta, and Pinterest campaigns with full UTM tracking and call tracking. Set up inquiry form notifications and CRM tags so your team responds within minutes.

5
Seasonal Budget Scaling

Scale budget aggressively during December 26 – February 14 (peak engagement season) and March–May (spring booking surge). Pull back during low-inquiry periods to conserve budget for when couples are actively deciding.

6
Attribution & Calendar Reporting

Monthly reports showing inquiries, tours, deposits, and booked weddings by channel. Track cost-per-booked-wedding to understand true marketing ROI — not just cost-per-click or cost-per-inquiry.

Wedding Marketing Questions

Google Search captures couples actively searching for your category (e.g., 'NYC wedding photographer under $5K'). Pinterest drives early-stage inspiration and warm traffic that converts on retargeting. Meta is effective for retargeting venue visitors and running lookalike audiences based on past inquiries. Directory presence (The Knot, WeddingWire) and reviews matter enormously — we optimize all channels in a unified strategy.

Wedding marketing has two major peaks: engagement season (December 25 – February 14) when newly engaged couples start vendor research, and spring (March–June) when couples ramp up booking for September–November weddings. We build a 12-month calendar that front-loads budget during peak inquiry seasons, pulls back during low-intent periods, and runs branding campaigns year-round to maintain search presence.

Wedding venues need a volume of inquiry forms and tour bookings from couples whose budget, guest count, and date align with availability. We run Google Search ads targeting high-intent queries ('wedding venue New York with outdoor space'), Meta lookalike campaigns based on past booked couples, and Pinterest campaigns that drive venue discovery. We track every inquiry to its ad source so you know exactly which channels fill your calendar.

The Knot and WeddingWire are high-intent discovery platforms with strong SEO authority. Couples who find you on these platforms are often further along in their search. We help optimize your directory profiles (photography, pricing tiers, reviews), manage review generation campaigns, and use directories as retargeting audiences on Meta — serving ads to couples who viewed your profile but haven't inquired.

Wedding marketing campaigns typically run $1,500–4,000/month in management fees depending on channels and vendor type, plus ad spend. For a NYC wedding venue, we target $150–400 cost-per-qualified-inquiry and a 20–35% inquiry-to-tour conversion rate. Photographers and planners typically see $80–200 CPL. Results peak 60–90 days after launch as ad accounts optimize and retargeting pools build.
