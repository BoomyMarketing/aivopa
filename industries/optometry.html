Optometry Marketing Questions

Google Search is the primary channel — patients searching "eye exam near me NYC" or "optometrist accepting VSP Brooklyn" have specific intent and convert at high rates. Google Business Profile and local SEO are critical for neighborhood-level visibility in a city where patients choose the closest provider who accepts their insurance. Meta ads work for retargeting lapsed patients who haven't had an exam in 12–24 months and for promoting specialty services like dry eye treatment or myopia management. Email recall campaigns to existing patients are one of the highest-ROI activities for optometry practices that have a patient database.
NYC optometry is hyperlocal — patients walk or take one subway stop to their eye doctor. We build campaigns with tight geographic targeting around each practice location, optimized GBP with insurance accepted prominently displayed, and review generation that builds trust before a first appointment. Insurance acceptance targeting ("accepting VSP," "EyeMed provider NYC") is one of the highest-converting filters in optometry Google Search — patients who are limited to in-network providers convert immediately when they confirm coverage. Online scheduling is now expected in NYC; practices without it lose appointments to competitors.
October–December is the highest-demand period for optometry in NYC as patients rush to use expiring vision benefits before December 31. We run dedicated end-of-year benefit reminder campaigns in October and November targeting existing patients and new patients searching "use vision benefits before year end NYC." Email campaigns to the patient database with "your vision benefits expire December 31" messaging drive significant recall appointment volume at near-zero acquisition cost. Google Search campaigns targeting "vision benefits expiring" and "eye exam before December" capture new patients in the same window.
Dry eye treatment, orthokeratology (Ortho-K), myopia management for children, and specialty contact lens fitting are all high-value services that attract patients specifically searching for them — and patients who accept them are less price-sensitive than general exam patients. We build dedicated landing pages and campaigns for each specialty: "dry eye specialist NYC," "myopia control for kids Brooklyn," "scleral lens fitting Manhattan." Specialty campaigns typically generate higher-revenue patients and reduce price shopping because the service is specialized enough that patients don't comparison-shop on price alone.

With average annual revenue per active patient running $300–600 and patients returning yearly for exams plus eyewear purchases, LTV per acquired patient is strong.
