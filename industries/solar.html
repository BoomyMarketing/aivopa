Our Process

How We Build Your Solar Lead Pipeline in 60 Days

1
Service Area & Customer Profile

Map your installation service area by county and zip code. Define your ideal customer: homeowner, monthly electricity bill $150+, roof age under 15 years, suitable sun exposure. Build exclusion lists for renters, condos, and areas outside your service radius.

2
Incentive Messaging Strategy

Build a campaign messaging framework around NY solar incentives. Create landing pages with savings calculator, incentive breakdown, and "get your free quote" CTA. Incentive-specific messaging drives 2–3x higher click-through rates than generic solar ads.

3
Multi-Channel Launch

Launch Google Search for high-intent queries, Meta for homeowner prospecting, and YouTube for education-phase awareness simultaneously. Each channel serves a different funnel stage and feeds retargeting audiences for the others.

4
Lead Qualification Setup

Configure multi-step lead forms with homeownership, roof type, electricity bill, and shade questions. CRM integration with lead scoring. Automated immediate follow-up sequence (email + SMS) for new leads — speed-to-contact is the #1 predictor of solar lead conversion.

5
Nurture Sequences

Build 8–12 week email education sequences for leads who don't convert immediately — covering solar ROI, incentives, financing options, and customer testimonials. Retargeting campaigns maintain brand visibility during the 30–90 day consideration window.

6
Cost-Per-Installed-Job Optimization

Monthly reporting on leads, appointments, proposals, and closed-won revenue by channel. Shift budget toward channels with the lowest cost-per-installed-job — not just lowest CPL. High-quality leads at higher CPL often outperform cheap leads with poor contact rates.
