What We Cover

Insurance Marketing Channels

Insurance advertising is dominated by carriers with eight-figure ad budgets. Independent agencies and brokers can't win on head-to-head brand terms — but they can win on local trust, specialty expertise, and long-tail queries where the carrier ad machine doesn't bother to compete.

🔍

Google Search — Long-Tail & Local

Focus on queries where local agencies win: "[insurance type] agent near me", "independent insurance broker NYC", "[niche] insurance New York", and specialty coverage queries. Avoid head-to-head bidding against GEICO and Progressive on generic terms — that's a budget drain with no competitive advantage.

🏢

Commercial Insurance B2B

LinkedIn ABM targeting business owners, CFOs, and Operations Directors at companies in your target industries. Google Search for commercial insurance intent: "business insurance quote NYC", "general liability insurance contractor NY", "professional liability insurance [industry]".

📱

Meta — Lifecycle & Referral

Retargeting website visitors who requested quotes but didn't bind. Referral program promotion to existing policyholders. Life insurance campaigns targeting life events (new parents, new homeowners, business owners) via Facebook interest and life event targeting.

🗺️

Local SEO & Google Maps

GBP optimization for "[insurance type] agent [borough/neighborhood]" searches. Local citations on Yelp, Angi, and insurance directories. Review generation strategy — local agency trust signals matter when competing against direct-to-consumer carrier websites.

📞

Call Tracking & Lead Scoring

CallRail tracking with call recording and disposition tagging (quote requested, bound, not qualified). CRM integration to track leads from first contact through bound policy. We optimize on bound policies — not quote volume — because a cheap lead that never binds is worthless.

📧

Email Nurture & Renewal

Multi-touch email sequences for open quotes (following up on quote requests that didn't bind), policy renewal reminder campaigns, and cross-sell sequences for policyholders with single-line policies. Renewal retention reduces CAC over the lifetime of a policyholder relationship.

Our Process

How We Grow Insurance Agencies

Insurance marketing is won in the details — keyword selection, compliance, lead quality, and retention. We build for long-term policyholder economics, not just quote volume.

1

Lines of Business & Competitive Audit

Map your top lines of business by premium volume, commission rate, and retention. Identify the 3–5 specialty areas where your agency has an expertise advantage over carriers. These become the anchor of your keyword strategy — competing on expertise, not price.

2

Google Ads Compliance Setup

Verify Google insurance advertiser certification. Review state licensing requirements for advertising in NY. Build ad copy templates with required disclosures. Set up automated policy violation alerts — insurance ads get disapproved frequently, and catching them immediately prevents lead flow interruption.

3

Campaign Architecture

Separate campaigns by line of business (auto, home, commercial, life, specialty). Long-tail keyword strategy focusing on local, specialty, and service-quality queries. Aggressive negative keyword list excluding research, jobs, and claims queries that waste budget.

4

Landing Pages by Line

Separate landing pages per insurance product: clear value prop (independent vs. captive, specialty expertise, local service), quote form, and trust signals (years in business, carrier partners, reviews). Personalization to match the ad query to the page content raises Quality Score and conversion rate simultaneously.

5

CRM & Policy Attribution

Connect ad platforms to your AMS (Applied Epic, AMS360, or EZLynx) to track which campaigns produce bound policies. Import offline conversion data back to Google Ads to train Smart Bidding on bound policies. This shift from CPL to cost-per-bound-policy optimization typically reduces wasted spend by 30–40%.

6

Renewal & Cross-Sell Programs

Email sequences for policyholders approaching renewal (60 days, 30 days, 15 days before expiry). Cross-sell sequences identifying single-line policyholders who may need additional coverage. Multi-line policyholders have 3x higher retention — cross-sell is the highest-ROI retention activity.

FAQ

Insurance Marketing Questions

What insurance lines do you work with?

Auto insurance, home and renters insurance, life insurance, health insurance, commercial and business insurance, professional liability (E&O, D&O), cyber liability, and specialty lines. Each line has different CPCs, buyer behavior, and compliance requirements — we build separate campaigns per line of business.

How competitive is insurance advertising on Google?

Insurance is one of the most expensive Google Ads categories globally. Auto insurance CPCs in NYC run $15–$60 per click; life insurance $25–$80; commercial insurance $20–$70. Competing on head-to-head terms against carriers is often not cost-effective for independent agencies — we focus on long-tail, local, and specialty queries where your agency's local expertise and personalized service is the differentiator.

Do you need a special license to advertise insurance on Google?

Google requires insurance advertisers to be licensed in the states where they advertise and to comply with state-specific disclosure requirements. We handle the Google certification process for your account and ensure all ad copy includes required disclosures. Non-compliant insurance ads get disapproved or accounts suspended — we prevent that.

How do you track lead quality for insurance campaigns?

We implement call tracking (CallRail) with call recording, form submission tracking with policy type classification, and CRM integration (Applied Epic, AMS360, or HubSpot) to track which leads convert to bound policies. Optimizing on bound policies rather than quote requests is what drives profitable insurance marketing.

Can you help with both personal lines and commercial insurance marketing?

Yes, but with very different strategies. Personal lines (auto, home, life) use consumer-facing Google Search and Meta campaigns with broad local targeting. Commercial insurance uses B2B tactics — LinkedIn ABM targeting business owners and CFOs, Google Search for business insurance queries, and account-based email sequences. We build separate campaign structures for each.
