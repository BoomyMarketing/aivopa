Pest Control Marketing Questions

Google Search and LSA are the top channels for pest control — NYC residents and property managers searching 'exterminator near me,' 'bed bug exterminator NYC,' 'cockroach exterminator Brooklyn,' or 'rat control Manhattan' are in urgent need and call within minutes. LSA (Google Guaranteed) appears at the very top of search results and charges per lead, not per click — making it highly cost-efficient for pest control. Local SEO and Google Business Profile generate organic calls for neighborhood-specific pest queries. Commercial pest control (restaurants, hotels, property management) requires LinkedIn and direct outreach to facility managers and property owners for larger contract acquisition.

New York has a distinct pest profile that drives specific high-volume search queries. Bed bugs are the #1 highest-CPL and highest-urgency pest in NYC — bed bug treatment searches spike year-round and convert within hours. Cockroaches and mice are the highest-volume residential searches. Rats are a growing NYC-specific issue with high search volume especially in outer boroughs. Seasonal pests: ants and termites in spring/summer, mice in fall/winter when they move indoors. We build dedicated campaigns and landing pages for each major pest — a homeowner searching 'bed bug exterminator' should land on a bed-bug-specific page, not a generic exterminator homepage.

Commercial pest control accounts — restaurants, hotels, apartment buildings, offices — are the highest-LTV clients for NYC exterminators. A single restaurant pest control contract ($200–600/month) generates $2,400–7,200 annually, and property management companies managing multi-unit buildings can be worth $50,000–200,000+ per year in recurring service. Commercial prospects are reached through LinkedIn targeting facility managers and property owners, Google Search for 'commercial pest control NYC' and 'restaurant exterminator New York,' and direct outreach to hospitality and property management companies. NYC Health Department inspections and violations create urgency that drives commercial pest control decisions.

Bed bug treatment marketing is distinct because of the extreme urgency and shame associated with bed bugs — clients call immediately, often same day, and price shop less than for any other pest. We build dedicated bed bug campaigns with 24/7 coverage, specific bed bug landing pages with heat treatment and chemical options explained, and clear pricing transparency (NYC bed bug treatment costs $500–3,000+ per unit). Bed bug search volume in NYC is highest in summer (peak travel/hotel exposure season) but remains significant year-round. Discretion messaging ('confidential service, unmarked vans') significantly improves conversion rates for bed bug clients.

Google Search for pest control keywords in NYC costs $5–20 per click (bed bug keywords are the most expensive), with CPL ranging from $30–100. LSA charges $15–50 per qualified lead — often the most efficient paid channel for residential pest calls. Most pest control companies start with $800–2,500/month in ad spend and see 20–60 qualified leads per month. Residential jobs average $150–1,500 (mice/roaches to bed bug heat treatment), commercial contracts $2,400–50,000+ annually. Even at $60 CPL, a single bed bug treatment ($800–1,500) delivers immediate ROI. We track calls, service appointments, and revenue by channel.
