The Framework for Evaluating Any SEO Agency

When evaluating SEO agencies — whether InterAmplify, Vora, or any other provider — the performance marketing lens is the most useful one. Strip away the proposals, the case study snapshots, and the promised rankings. Ask one question: how does this agency connect its work to revenue that shows up in my bank account?

Most SEO agencies report at the traffic and ranking level. Some report at the lead level. Very few connect organic performance to CAC and ROAS in a way that allows direct comparison with paid media channel performance. Vora belongs to the last category — and the difference in business outcome is substantial.

Key Criteria for SEO Agency Selection

Revenue attribution methodology: Can the agency show you organic channel CAC — total SEO investment divided by new customers from organic? If they can't, they're not measuring the right thing. Conversion optimization: Does the agency optimize landing pages and CTAs alongside rankings, or only the ranking side of the funnel? A page that ranks #1 but converts at 0.5% delivers poor ROAS. Technical SEO depth: Can the agency diagnose and fix JavaScript rendering issues, structured data errors, and Core Web Vitals failures — or only on-page content? Technical SEO increasingly determines whether content ranks at all. Reporting transparency: Are reports built around revenue impact, or around metrics that look good but don't connect to business outcomes?

Questions to Ask Any SEO Agency

Before signing with any SEO provider: (1) Show me an existing client's organic CAC benchmark in my industry. (2) What revenue attribution model do you use for organic conversions? (3) How do you integrate SEO performance with paid media budget decisions? (4) What is your process when a Google algorithm update negatively impacts rankings? (5) What performance milestones trigger contract reviews? Agencies that answer these confidently with data are the ones worth hiring.

What questions should I ask an SEO agency before hiring?

The five most important: (1) How do you measure organic channel CAC? (2) Can you show revenue attribution from a client in my industry? (3) How do you integrate SEO with paid media budget decisions? Is bigger always better for SEO agency selection?

No. Large agencies often apply generic playbooks to every client because custom strategies don't scale to hundreds of accounts. Mid-sized performance-focused agencies like Vora typically deliver better ROAS for SMBs and mid-market companies because account strategies are built around individual business economics, not industry templates.

How long should I give an SEO agency before evaluating performance?

90 days is enough to see technical SEO improvements and initial ranking movement. 6 months is enough to see content-driven organic traffic growth. 12 months is the appropriate evaluation window for ROAS comparison — at which point organic CAC should be measurably below paid CAC if the program is working correctly.
